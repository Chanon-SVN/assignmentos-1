==Events==
*[[43 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Forum Gallorum]]: [[Mark Antony]], besieging [[Julius Caesar|Caesar]]'s assassin [[Decimus Junius Brutus Albinus|Decimus Brutus]] in [[Mutina]], defeats the forces of the consul [[Gaius Vibius Pansa Caetronianus|Pansa]], but is then immediately defeated by the army of the other consul, [[Hirtius]].
* [[69]] &amp;ndash; [[Vitellius]], [[commander]] of the [[Rhine]] armies, defeats [[Roman Emperor|Emperor]] [[Otho]] in the [[Battle of Bedriacum]] and seizes the throne.
* [[70]] &amp;ndash; [[Siege of Jerusalem (70)|Siege of Jerusalem]]: [[Titus]], son of emperor [[Vespasian]], surrounds the Jewish capital, with four [[Roman legion]]s.
* [[193]] &amp;ndash; [[Septimius Severus]] is proclaimed [[Roman Emperor]] by the army in [[Illyricum (Roman province)|Illyricum]] (in the [[Balkans]]).
* [[966]] &amp;ndash; After his marriage to the [[Christian]] [[Dobrawa of Bohemia]], the [[paganism|pagan]] ruler of the [[Polans (western)|Polans]], [[Mieszko I of Poland|Mieszko&amp;nbsp;I]], [[Baptism of Poland|converts to Christianity]], an event considered to be the [[History of Poland during the Piast dynasty|founding of the Polish state]].
* [[972]] &amp;ndash; Emperor Otto II married Princess Theophano, niece of Byzantine Emperor John Tzimisces, leading to a significant impact on German intellectual life, including interest in Greek literature and Byzantine art.
*[[1028]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry III, Holy Roman Emperor|Henry III]], son of [[Conrad II, Holy Roman Emperor|Conrad]], is elected [[King of Germany|king]] of the [[Germany|Germans]].
*[[1205]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Adrianople (1205)|Battle of Adrianople]] between [[Bulgarians]] and [[Crusaders]].
*[[1294]] &amp;ndash; [[Temür Khan, Emperor Chengzong of Yuan|Temür]], grandson of [[Kublai]], is elected [[Khagan]] of the [[Mongols]] and Emperor of the [[Yuan Dynasty]] with the reigning titles Oljeitu and Chengzong.
*[[1341]] &amp;ndash; Sack of [[Saluzzo]] (Italy) by Italian-Angevine troops under [[Manfred V of Saluzzo]].
*[[1434]] &amp;ndash; The foundation stone of [[Cathedral of St. Peter and St. Paul, Nantes|Cathedral St. Peter and St. Paul]] in [[Nantes]], France is laid.
*[[1471]] &amp;ndash; In England, the [[Yorkists]] under [[Edward IV of England|Edward IV]] defeat the [[House of Lancaster|Lancastrians]] under the [[Richard Neville, 16th Earl of Warwick|Earl of Warwick]] at the [[Battle of Barnet]]; the Earl is killed and [[Edward IV]] resumes the throne.
*[[1639]] &amp;ndash; Imperial forces are defeated by the Swedes at the [[Battle of Chemnitz]]. The Swedish victory prolongs the [[Thirty Years' War]] and allows them to advance into [[Bohemia]].
*[[1699]] &amp;ndash; [[Khalsa]]: The [[Sikh]] religion was formalised as the [[Khalsa]] - the brotherhood of Warrior-Saints - by [[Guru Gobind Singh]] in [[Northern India]], in accordance with the [[Nanakshahi calendar]].
*[[1715]] &amp;ndash; The [[Yamasee War]] begins in [[South Carolina]].
*[[1775]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Abolitionism in the United States|abolition]] society in North America is established. The [[Pennsylvania Abolition Society|Society for the Relief of Free Negroes Unlawfully Held in Bondage]] is organized in [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]] by [[Benjamin Franklin]] and [[Benjamin Rush]].
*[[1816]] &amp;ndash; [[Bussa]], a slave in British-ruled [[Barbados]], leads a slave rebellion and is killed. For this, he is remembered as the first national hero of Barbados.
*[[1828]] &amp;ndash; [[Noah Webster]] copyrights the first edition of his [[Webster's Dictionary|dictionary]].
*[[1846]] &amp;ndash; The [[Donner Party timeline|Donner Party]] of [[Settler|pioneers]] departs [[Springfield, Illinois]], for [[California]], on what will become a year-long journey of hardship, [[cannibalism]], and survival.
*[[1849]] &amp;ndash; Hungary [[Hungarian Declaration of Independence|declares itself independent]] of [[Austrian Empire|Austria]] with [[Lajos Kossuth]] as its leader.
*[[1860]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Pony Express]] rider reaches [[San Francisco]]. The pony riders carried additionally, along with the mail, [[Pony Express Bible|a small personal bible]].
*[[1865]] &amp;ndash; U.S. President [[Abraham Lincoln]] is [[Abraham Lincoln assassination|shot]] in [[Ford's Theatre]] by [[John Wilkes Booth]] (died April 15th).
*  1865   &amp;ndash; [[United States Secretary of State|U.S. Secretary of State]] [[William H. Seward]] and his family are attacked in his home by [[Lewis Powell (assassin)|Lewis Powell]].
*[[1881]] &amp;ndash; The [[Four Dead in Five Seconds Gunfight]] is fought in [[El Paso, Texas]].
*[[1890]] &amp;ndash; The [[Pan-American Union]] is founded by the First [[International Conference of American States]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]
*[[1894]] &amp;ndash; The first ever commercial [[film|motion picture]] house opened in New York City using ten [[Kinetoscope]]s, a device for peep-show viewing of films.
*[[1906]] &amp;ndash; The [[Azusa Street Revival]] opens and will launch [[Pentecostalism]] as a worldwide movement.
*[[1909]] &amp;ndash; [[Adana massacre|A massacre]] is organized by [[Ottoman Empire]] against Armenian population of [[Cilicia]].
*[[1912]] &amp;ndash; The British passenger liner {{RMS|Titanic}} hits an iceberg in the [[North Atlantic]] at 23:40 (sinks morning of April 15th).
*[[1927]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Volvo]] [[automobile|car]] premieres in [[Gothenburg]], Sweden.
*[[1928]] &amp;ndash; The [[Bremen (aircraft)|Bremen]], a German [[Junkers W33]] type aircraft, reaches [[Greenly Island, Canada]] - the first successful transatlantic aeroplane flight from east to west.
*[[1931]] &amp;ndash; The [[Cortes Generales|Spanish Cortes]] deposes King [[Alfonso XIII of Spain|Alfonso XIII]] and proclaims the [[Second Spanish Republic]].
*  1931   &amp;ndash; First edition of the [[Highway Code]] published in Great Britain.
*[[1935]] &amp;ndash; &quot;[[Black Sunday (storm)|Black Sunday Storm]]&quot;, the worst dust storm of the U.S. [[Dust Bowl]].
*[[1939]] &amp;ndash; ''[[The Grapes of Wrath]]'', by American author [[John Steinbeck]] is first published by the [[Viking Press]].
*[[1940]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Royal Marines]] land in [[Namsos]], Norway in preparation for a larger force to arrive two days later.
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; World War II: German general [[Erwin Rommel]] attacks [[Siege of Tobruk|Tobruk]].
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; [[Malta]] receives the [[George Cross]] for its gallantry. The [[George Cross]] was given by King [[George VI]] himself and is now an [[emblem]] on the [[Flag of Malta|Maltese national flag]].
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; [[Bombay Explosion (1944)|Bombay Explosion]]: A massive [[explosion]] in [[Bombay]] [[harbor]] kills 300 and causes economic damage valued then at 20 million pounds.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; [[Osijek]], [[Croatia]], is liberated from [[fascist]] occupation.
*[[1956]] &amp;ndash; In [[Chicago]], [[Quadruplex videotape|videotape]] is first demonstrated.
*[[1958]] &amp;ndash; The [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] [[satellite]] [[Sputnik 2]] falls from [[orbit]] after a mission duration of 162 days.
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; [[Gnassingbé Eyadéma]] overthrows [[President of Togo]] [[Nicolas Grunitzky]] and installs himself as the new president, a title he would hold for the next 38 years.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; At the U.S. [[Academy Awards]] there is a tie for the [[Academy Award for Best Actress]] between [[Katharine Hepburn]] and [[Barbra Streisand]].
*[[1978]] &amp;ndash; [[1978 Tbilisi Demonstrations]]: Thousands of [[Georgia (country)|Georgians]] demonstrate against Soviet attempts to change the constitutional status of the [[Georgian language]].
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; [[STS-1]] &amp;ndash; The first operational [[space shuttle]], [[Space Shuttle Columbia|Columbia]] (OV-102) completes its first test flight.
*[[1986]] &amp;ndash; In retaliation for the [[April 5]] [[1986 Berlin discotheque bombing|bombing]] in [[West Berlin]] that killed two U.S.  servicemen, U.S. president [[Ronald Reagan]] orders [[Operation El Dorado Canyon|major bombing raids]] against [[Libya]], killing 60 people.
*  1986   &amp;ndash; The heaviest [[hailstone]]s ever recorded ({{convert|1|kg|lb}}) fall on the [[Gopalganj District (Bangladesh)|Gopalganj district of Bangladesh]], killing 92.
*[[1988]] &amp;ndash; The {{USS|Samuel B. Roberts|FFG-58|6}} strikes a [[naval mine|mine]] in the [[Persian Gulf]] during [[Operation Earnest Will]].
*  1988   &amp;ndash; In a [[United Nations]] ceremony in [[Geneva]], Switzerland, the Soviet Union signs an agreement pledging to withdraw its troops from [[Afghanistan]].
*[[1991]] &amp;ndash; The [[Georgia (country)|Republic of Georgia]] introduces the post of [[President of Georgia|President]] after its declaration of independence from the Soviet Union.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; In a U.S. [[friendly fire]] incident during [[Operation Provide Comfort]] in northern [[Iraq]], two [[United States Air Force]] aircraft mistakenly [[Black Hawk Incident (April 1994)|shoot-down]] two [[United States Army]] [[helicopter]]s, killing 26 people.
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; [[NATO]] mistakenly bombs a convoy of ethnic [[Albania]]n refugees &amp;ndash; [[Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|Yugoslav]] officials say 75 people were killed.
*  1999   &amp;ndash; A [[1999 Sydney hailstorm|severe hailstorm]] strikes Sydney, Australia causing [[Australian dollar|A$]]2.3 billion in insured damages, the most costly natural disaster in Australian history.
*[[2002]] &amp;ndash; [[Venezuela]]n President [[Hugo Chávez]] returns to office two days after being ousted and arrested by the country's military.
*[[2003]] &amp;ndash; The [[Human Genome Project]] is completed with 99% of the human [[genome]] sequenced to an accuracy of 99.99%.
*  2003   &amp;ndash; U.S. troops in [[Baghdad]] capture [[Abu Abbas]], leader of the [[Palestinian people|Palestinian]] group that killed an American on the hijacked cruise liner the {{MS|Achille Lauro}} in [[1985]].
*[[2005]] &amp;ndash; The [[Oregon Supreme Court]] nullifies marriage licenses issued to gay couples a year earlier by [[Multnomah County, Oregon|Multnomah County]].
*[[2007]] &amp;ndash; [[Republic Protests|At least 200,000 demonstrators]] in [[Ankara]], Turkey, protest against the possible candidacy of incumbent Prime Minister [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]].
*[[2010]] &amp;ndash; Nearly 2,700 are killed in a [[2010 Yushu earthquake|magnitude 6.9 earthquake]] in [[Yushu Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture|Yushu]], [[Qinghai]], China.
*[[2014]] &amp;ndash; [[April 2014 Abuja bombing|Twin bomb blasts]] in [[Abuja]], [[Nigeria]], kill at least 75 people and injures 141 others.
