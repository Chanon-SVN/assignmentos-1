==Events==
* [[217]] &amp;ndash; [[Roman Emperor]] [[Caracalla]] is [[assassinated]]. He is succeeded by his [[Praetorian Guard]] [[prefect]], [[Marcus Opellius Macrinus]].
* [[632]] &amp;ndash; King [[Charibert II]] is assassinated at [[Blaye]] ([[Gironde]])—possibly on orders of his half-brother [[Dagobert I]]—along with his infant son [[Chilperic of Aquitaine|Chilperic]]. He claims [[Aquitaine]] and [[Gascony]], becoming the most powerful [[Merovingian dynasty|Merovingian]] king in the West.
* [[876]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Dayr al-'Aqul]] saves [[Baghdad]] from the [[Saffarids]].
*[[1093]] &amp;ndash; The new [[Winchester Cathedral]] is dedicated by [[Walkelin]].
*[[1139]] &amp;ndash; [[Roger II of Sicily]] is excommunicated.
*[[1149]] &amp;ndash; [[Pope Eugene III]] takes refuge in the castle of [[Ptolemy II of Tusculum]].
*[[1232]] &amp;ndash; [[Mongol conquest of the Jin Dynasty|Mongol–Jin War]]: The [[Mongol Empire|Mongols]] begin their [[Mongol siege of Kaifeng|siege on Kaifeng]], the capital of the [[Jin Dynasty (1115–1234)|Jin Dynasty]].
*[[1271]] &amp;ndash; In [[Syria]], sultan [[Baybars]] conquers the [[Krak of Chevaliers]].
*[[1665]] &amp;ndash; English colonial patents are granted for the establishment of the [[Monmouth Tract]], for what would eventually become [[Monmouth County]] in northeastern New Jersey.
*[[1730]] &amp;ndash; [[Shearith Israel]], the first [[synagogue]] in [[New York City]], is dedicated.
*[[1740]] &amp;ndash; [[War of Jenkins' Ear]]: Three [[Kingdom of Great Britain|British]] ships [[Action of 8 April 1740|capture]] the [[Spain|Spanish]] [[third-rate]] {{HMS|Princess|1740|6}}.
*[[1808]] &amp;ndash; The [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Baltimore|Roman Catholic Diocese of Baltimore]] is promoted to an archdiocese, with the founding of the dioceses of [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York|New York]], [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Philadelphia|Philadelphia]], [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Boston|Boston]], and [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Louisville|Bardstown]] (now Louisville) by [[Pope Pius VII]].
*[[1820]] &amp;ndash; The [[Venus de Milo]] is discovered on the [[Aegean Sea|Aegean]] island of [[Melos]].
*[[1832]] &amp;ndash; [[Black Hawk War]]: Around three-hundred [[United States]] 6th Infantry troops leave [[St. Louis, Missouri]] to fight the [[Sauk people|Sauk]] [[Native Americans in the United States|Native Americans]].
*[[1864]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Battle of Mansfield]] &amp;ndash; [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] forces are thwarted by the [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] army at [[Mansfield, Louisiana]].
*[[1866]] &amp;ndash; [[Italy]] and [[Prussia]] [[Austro-Prussian War|ally against]] the [[Austrian Empire]].
*[[1886]] &amp;ndash; [[William Ewart Gladstone]] introduces the first [[Irish Government Bill 1886|Irish Home Rule Bill]] into the [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom|British House of Commons]].
*[[1895]] &amp;ndash; In [[Pollock v. Farmers' Loan &amp; Trust Co.]] the [[Supreme Court of the United States]] declares unapportioned [[income tax]] to be [[Constitutionality|unconstitutional]].
*[[1904]] &amp;ndash; The [[French Third Republic]] and the [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland]] sign the ''[[Entente cordiale]]''.
*  1904   &amp;ndash; [[United Kingdom|British]] mystic [[Aleister Crowley]] transcribes the first chapter of ''[[The Book of the Law]]''.
*  1904   &amp;ndash; [[Longacre Square]] in [[Midtown Manhattan]] is renamed [[Times Square]] after ''[[The New York Times]]''.
*[[1906]] &amp;ndash; [[Auguste Deter]], the first person to be diagnosed with [[Alzheimer's disease]], dies.
*[[1908]] &amp;ndash; [[Harvard University]] votes to establish the [[Harvard Business School]].
*[[1911]] &amp;ndash; [[Dutch people|Dutch]] physicist [[Heike Kamerlingh Onnes]] discovers [[superconductivity]].
*[[1913]] &amp;ndash; The [[17th Amendment to the United States Constitution]], requiring direct [[election]] of [[United States Senate|Senators]], becomes law.
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; In [[Corona, California]], race car driver [[Bob Burman]] crashes, killing three, and badly injuring five, spectators.
*[[1918]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: [[Actor]]s [[Douglas Fairbanks]] and [[Charlie Chaplin]] sell [[war bond]]s on the streets of New York City's financial district.
*[[1924]] &amp;ndash; [[Sharia]] courts are abolished in [[Turkey]], as part of [[Atatürk's Reforms]].
*[[1929]] &amp;ndash; [[Indian Independence Movement]]: At the Delhi Central Assembly, [[Bhagat Singh]] and [[Batukeshwar Dutt]] throw handouts and bombs to court arrest.
*[[1935]] &amp;ndash; The [[Works Progress Administration]] is formed when the [[Emergency Relief Appropriation Act of 1935]] becomes law.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Siege of Leningrad]] &amp;ndash; [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] forces open a much-needed [[railway]] link to [[Saint Petersburg|Leningrad]].
*  1942   &amp;ndash; World War II: The [[Empire of Japan|Japanese]] take [[Bataan]] in the [[Philippines]].
*[[1943]] &amp;ndash; [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Franklin D. Roosevelt]], in an attempt to check [[inflation]], freezes wages and prices, prohibits workers from changing jobs unless the [[war]] effort would be aided thereby, and bars rate increases by [[common carrier]]s and [[public utilities]].
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; World War II: After an air raid accidentally destroys a train carrying about 4,000 [[Nazi concentration camp]] internees in [[Prussian Hanover]], the survivors are [[Celler Hasenjagd|massacred]] by Nazis.
*[[1946]] &amp;ndash; [[Électricité de France]], the world's largest [[utility company]], is formed as a result of the [[nationalisation]] of a number of electricity producers, transporters and distributors.
*[[1950]] &amp;ndash; [[India]] and [[Pakistan]] sign the [[Liaquat-Nehru Pact]].
*[[1952]] &amp;ndash; U.S. President [[Harry Truman]] calls for the seizure of all domestic [[steel mill]]s to prevent a nationwide [[Strike action|strike]].
*[[1953]] &amp;ndash; [[Mau Mau]] leader [[Jomo Kenyatta]] is convicted by [[Kenya]]'s [[United Kingdom|British]] rulers.
*[[1954]] &amp;ndash; A Royal Canadian Air Force Canadair [[T-6 Texan|Harvard]] collides with a Trans-Canada Airlines [[Canadair North Star]] over [[Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan]], killing 37 people.
*  1954   &amp;ndash; [[South African Airways Flight 201]] A [[De Havilland Comet|de Havilland DH.106 Comet 1]] crashes into the sea during night killing 21 people.
*[[1959]] &amp;ndash; A team of computer manufacturers, users, and university people led by [[Grace Hopper]] meets to discuss the creation of a new [[programming language]] that would be called [[COBOL]].
*  1959   &amp;ndash; The [[Organization of American States]] drafts an agreement to create the [[Inter-American Development Bank]].
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; The [[Netherlands]] and [[West Germany]] sign an agreement to negotiate the return of [[Dutch annexation of German territory after World War II|German land annexed by the Dutch]] in return for 280 million [[German mark]]s as [[Wiedergutmachung]].
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; A large explosion on board the {{MV|Dara}} in the [[Persian Gulf]] kills 238.
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; [[Gemini 1]] (unamanned test flight) launched.
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; [[BOAC Flight 712]] catches fire shortly after take off. As a result of her actions in the accident, [[Barbara Jane Harrison]] is awarded a posthumous [[George Cross]], the only GC awarded to a woman in peacetime.
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; [[Bahr el-Baqar incident]]: Israeli bombers strike an Egyptian school. 46 children are killed.
*[[1974]] &amp;ndash; At [[Atlanta-Fulton County Stadium]], [[Hank Aaron]] hits his 715th career [[home run]] to surpass [[Babe Ruth]]'s 39-year-old record.
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; [[Frank Robinson]] manages the [[Cleveland Indians]] in his first game as major league [[baseball]]'s first [[African American]] manager.
*[[1987]] &amp;ndash; [[Los Angeles Dodgers]] executive [[Al Campanis]] resigns amid controversy over [[Racism|racially charged]] remarks he had made while on ''[[Nightline (US news program)|Nightline]]''.
*[[1992]] &amp;ndash; Retired [[tennis]] great [[Arthur Ashe]] announces that he has [[AIDS]], acquired from [[blood transfusion]]s during one of his two heart surgeries.
&lt;!-- do not add Kurt Cobain's death.  Deaths go in the death section, this one is listed on April 5 --&gt;
*[[1993]] &amp;ndash; The [[Republic of Macedonia]] joins the [[United Nations]].
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; [[Haryana Gana Parishad]], a [[political party]] in the Indian state of [[Haryana]], merges with the [[Indian National Congress]].
*[[2004]] &amp;ndash; [[Darfur conflict]]: The [[April 8 Humanitarian Ceasefire Agreement|Humanitarian Ceasefire Agreement]] is signed by the [[Sudan]]ese [[government]] and two rebel groups.
*[[2005]] &amp;ndash; Over four million people attend the [[funeral of Pope John Paul II]].
*[[2006]] &amp;ndash; [[Shedden massacre]]: The bodies of eight men, all shot to death, are found in a field in [[Ontario, Canada]]. The murders are soon linked to the [[Bandidos]] motorcycle gang.
*[[2008]] &amp;ndash; The construction of the [[Bahrain World Trade Center|world's first building to integrate wind turbines]] is completed in [[Bahrain]].
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; The [[Islamic State of Iraq]] enters the [[Syrian Civil War]] and begins by declaring a merger with the [[Al-Nusra Front]] under the name Islamic State of Iraq and ash-Sham
