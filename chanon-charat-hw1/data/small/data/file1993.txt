The '''Brassicales''' are an [[order (biology)|order]] of [[flowering plant]]s, belonging to the [[eurosids II]] group of [[dicotyledon]]s under the [[APG II]] system.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal |author=[[Angiosperm Phylogeny Group]] |year=2003 |title=An update of the Angiosperm Phylogeny Group classification for the orders and families of flowering plants: APG II |journal=[[Botanical Journal of the Linnean Society]] |volume=141 |issue=4 |pages=399–436 |doi=10.1046/j.1095-8339.2003.t01-1-00158.x}}&lt;/ref&gt; One character common to many members of the order is the production of [[glucosinolate]] (mustard oil) compounds. Most systems of classification have included this order, although sometimes under the name '''Capparales''' (the name chosen depending on which is thought to have priority).&lt;ref name=&quot;hall2002&quot;&gt;{{cite journal |author=Jocelyn C. Hall, Kenneth J. Sytsma &amp; Hugh H. Iltis |year=2002 |title=Phylogeny of Capparaceae and Brassicaceae based on chloroplast sequence data |journal=[[American Journal of Botany]] |volume=89 |issue=11 |pages=1826–1842 |pmid=21665611 |doi=10.3732/ajb.89.11.1826}}&lt;/ref&gt;

The order typically contains the following families:&lt;ref name=&quot;Haston&quot;&gt;{{cite journal |author=Elspeth Haston, James E. Richardson, Peter F. Stevens, Mark W. Chase &amp; David J. Harris |year=2007 |title=A linear sequence of Angiosperm Phylogeny Group II families |journal=[[Taxon (journal)|Taxon]] |volume=56 |issue=1 |pages=7–12 |url=http://www.ingentaconnect.com/content/iapt/tax/2007/00000056/00000001/art00002}}&lt;/ref&gt;
* [[Akaniaceae]]
* [[Bataceae]] – salt-tolerant shrubs from America and [[Australasia]]
* [[Brassicaceae]] – [[Mustard plant|mustard]] and [[cabbage]] family; may include the [[Cleomaceae]]
* [[Capparaceae]] – [[caper]] family, sometimes included in [[Brassicaceae]]
* [[Caricaceae]] – [[papaya]] family
* [[Cleomaceae]]&lt;ref name=APGIII2009/&gt;
* [[Gyrostemonaceae]]
* [[Koeberliniaceae]]
* [[Limnanthaceae]] – [[meadowfoam]] family
* [[Moringa]]ceae – thirteen species of trees from Africa and India
* [[Pentadiplandraceae]]
* [[Resedaceae]] – [[Mignonette (Reseda)|mignonette]] family
* [[Salvadoraceae]]
* [[Setchellanthaceae]]
* [[Tovariaceae]]
* [[Tropaeolaceae]] – [[Tropaeolum|nasturtium]] family

Under the [[Cronquist system]], the Brassicales were called the Capparales, and included among the &quot;[[Dilleniidae]]&quot;.  The only families included were the [[Brassicaceae]] and [[Capparaceae]] (treated as separate families), the [[Tovariaceae]], [[Resedaceae]], and [[Moringaceae]].  Other taxa now included here were placed in various orders.

[[File:Cleome (Spider Flower) in Gavi.jpg|thumb|''[[Cleome hassleriana]]'']]

The families Capparaceae and Brassicaceae are closely related.  One group, consisting of ''[[Cleome]]'' and related genera, was traditionally included in the Capparaceae but doing so results in a [[paraphyletic]] Capparaceae.&lt;ref name=&quot;hall2002&quot;/&gt;  Therefore, this group is generally now either included in the Brassicaceae or as its own family, [[Cleomaceae]].&lt;ref name=&quot;Haston&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal |author=Jocelyn C. Hall, Hugh H. Iltis &amp; Kenneth J. Sytsma |year=2004 |title=Molecular phylogenetics of core Brassicales, placement of orphan genera ''Emblingia'', ''Forchhammeria'', ''Tirania'', and character evolution |journal=[[Systematic Botany]] |volume=29 |pages=654–669 |doi=10.1600/0363644041744491 |url=http://www.botany.wisc.edu/sytsma/LabWeb/pdf/Hall2004.pdf |format=[[Portable Document Format|PDF]]}}&lt;/ref&gt;
