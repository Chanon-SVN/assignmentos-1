==Events==
*[[1479 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Thutmose III]] ascends to the throne of [[Ancient Egypt|Egypt]], although power effectively shifts to [[Hatshepsut]] (according to the Low Chronology of the 18th Dynasty).
*[[1184 BC]] &amp;ndash; Traditional date of the [[Trojan_War#The Sack of Troy|fall of Troy]].
*[[1547]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Mühlberg]]. [[Fernando Álvarez de Toledo, 3rd Duke of Alba|Duke of Alba]], commanding Spanish-Imperial forces of [[Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor|Charles I of Spain]], defeats the troops of [[Schmalkaldic League]].
*[[1558]] &amp;ndash; [[Mary, Queen of Scots]], marries the [[Dauphin of France]], [[Francis II of France|François]], at [[Notre Dame de Paris]].
*[[1704]] &amp;ndash; The first regular [[newspaper]] in [[13 colonies|British Colonial America]], the ''News-Letter'', is published in [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]].
*[[1800]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States]] [[Library of Congress]] is established when [[President of the United States|President]] [[John Adams]] signs legislation to appropriate $5,000 [[United States dollar|USD]] to purchase &quot;such books as may be necessary for the use of [[Congress of the United States|Congress]]&quot;.
*[[1877]] &amp;ndash; [[Russo-Turkish War, 1877–1878|Russo-Turkish War]]: [[Russian Empire]] declares war on [[Ottoman Empire]].
*[[1885]] &amp;ndash; American [[sharpshooter]] [[Annie Oakley]] is hired by Nate Salsbury to be a part of [[Buffalo Bill's Wild West]].
*[[1895]] &amp;ndash; [[Joshua Slocum]], the first person to sail single-handedly around the world, sets sail from Boston, Massachusetts aboard the sloop &quot;Spray&quot;.
*[[1904]] &amp;ndash; The [[Lithuanian press ban]] is lifted after almost 40 years.
*[[1907]] &amp;ndash; [[Hersheypark]], founded by [[Milton S. Hershey]] for the exclusive use of his employees, is opened.
*[[1913]] &amp;ndash; The [[Woolworth Building]] [[skyscraper]] in [[New York City]] is opened.
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; The [[Franck–Hertz experiment]], a pillar of [[Introduction to quantum mechanics|quantum mechanics]], is presented to the [[Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft|German Physical Society]].
*[[1915]] &amp;ndash; The arrest of [[Armenian notables deported from the Ottoman capital in 1915|250 Armenian intellectuals and community leaders]] in [[Istanbul]] marks the beginning of the [[Armenian Genocide]].
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; [[Easter Rising]]: The [[Irish Republican Brotherhood]] led by [[nationalism|nationalists]] [[Patrick Pearse]], [[James Connolly]], and [[Joseph Plunkett]] starts a rebellion in [[Ireland]].
*  1916   &amp;ndash; [[Ernest Shackleton]] and five men of the [[Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition]] launch a lifeboat from uninhabited [[Elephant Island]] in the [[Southern Ocean]] to organise a rescue for the ice-trapped ship {{ship||Endurance|1912 ship|2}}.
*[[1918]] &amp;ndash; First tank-to-tank combat, at [[Villers-Bretonneux]], [[France]], when three British [[Mark IV tank|Mark IV]]s meet three [[Germany|German]] [[A7V]]s.
*[[1922]] &amp;ndash; The first segment of the [[Imperial Wireless Chain]] providing [[wireless telegraphy]] between [[Leafield]] in [[Oxfordshire]], [[England]], and [[Cairo]], [[Egypt]], comes into operation.
*[[1923]] &amp;ndash; In Vienna, the paper ''Das Ich und das Es'' ([[The Ego and the Id]]) by [[Sigmund Freud]] is published, which outlines Freud's theories of [[id, ego, and super-ego|the id, ego, and super-ego]].
*[[1926]] &amp;ndash; The [[Treaty of Berlin (1926)|Treaty of Berlin]] is signed. Germany and the [[Soviet Union]] each pledge neutrality in the event of an attack on the other by a third party for the next five years.
*[[1932]] &amp;ndash; [[Benny Rothman]] leads the [[mass trespass of Kinder Scout]], leading to substantial legal reforms in the United Kingdom.
*[[1933]] &amp;ndash; [[Nazi Germany]] begins its [[Persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses in Nazi Germany|persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses]] by shutting down the [[Corporations of Jehovah's Witnesses|Watch Tower Society]] office in [[Magdeburg]].
*[[1953]] &amp;ndash; [[Winston Churchill]] is knighted by [[Queen regnant|Queen]] [[Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom|Elizabeth II]].
*[[1955]] &amp;ndash; The [[Bandung Conference]] ends: Twenty-nine non-aligned nations of [[Asia]] and [[Africa]] finish a meeting that condemns [[colonialism]], [[racism]], and the [[Cold War]].
*[[1957]] &amp;ndash; [[Suez Crisis]]: The [[Suez Canal]] is reopened following the introduction of [[United Nations Emergency Force|UNEF]] peacekeepers to the region.
*  1957   &amp;ndash; The [[BBC]] first broadcast [[The Sky at Night]] presented by [[Patrick Moore]]
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; Marriage of HRH [[Princess Alexandra, The Honourable Lady Ogilvy|Princess Alexandra of Kent]] to the Hon [[Angus Ogilvy]] at [[Westminster Abbey]] in [[London]].
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; Civil war breaks out in the [[Dominican Republic]] when Colonel [[Francisco Caamaño]], overthrows the [[triumvirate]] that had been in power since the [[coup d'état]] against [[Juan Bosch]].
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; [[Astronaut|Cosmonaut]] [[Vladimir Mikhaylovich Komarov|Vladimir Komarov]] dies in [[Soyuz 1]] when its [[parachute]] fails to open. He is the first human to die during a space mission.
*  1967   &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: American [[General]] [[William Westmoreland]] says in a news conference that the enemy had &quot;gained support in the United States that gives him hope that he can win politically that which he cannot win militarily.&quot;
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; [[Mauritius]] becomes a member state of the [[United Nations]].
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; The first [[China|Chinese]] [[satellite]], [[Dong Fang Hong I]], is launched.
*  1970   &amp;ndash; [[The Gambia]] becomes a [[republic]] within the [[Commonwealth of Nations]], with [[Dawda Jawara]] as the first [[Heads of State of The Gambia|President]].
*[[1971]] &amp;ndash; [[Soyuz 10]] docks with Salyut 1.
*[[1980]] &amp;ndash; Eight U.S. servicemen die in [[Operation Eagle Claw]] as they attempt to end the [[Iran hostage crisis]].
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; [[STS-31]]: The [[Hubble Space Telescope]] is launched from the [[Space Shuttle program|Space Shuttle]] ''[[Space Shuttle Discovery|Discovery]]''. [[File:STS-31 Launch - GPN-2000-000684.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Shuttle mission [[STS-31]] lifts off, carrying [[Hubble Space Telescope|Hubble]] into orbit.]]
*  1990   &amp;ndash; [[Gruinard Island]], [[Scotland]], is officially declared free of the [[anthrax disease]] after 48 years of [[quarantine]].
*[[1993]] &amp;ndash; An [[Provisional Irish Republican Army|IRA]] [[1993 Bishopsgate bombing|bomb devastates the Bishopsgate area]] of London.
*[[1996]] &amp;ndash; In the United States, the [[Antiterrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act of 1996]] is passed into law.
*[[2004]] &amp;ndash; The United States lifts [[economic sanctions]] imposed on [[History of Libya under Muammar Gaddafi#Great Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya (1977–2012)|Libya]] 18 years previously, as a reward for its cooperation in eliminating [[weapons of mass destruction]].
*[[2005]] &amp;ndash; [[Cardinal (Catholicism)|Cardinal]] [[Joseph Ratzinger]] is [[Papal Inauguration|inaugurated]] as the 265th [[Pope]] of the [[Roman Catholic Church]] taking the name [[Pope Benedict XVI]].
*  2005   &amp;ndash; [[Snuppy]] becomes world's first [[Cloning|cloned]] [[dog]].
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; A [[2013 Savar building collapse|building collapses]] near [[Dhaka]], [[Bangladesh]], killing 1,129 people and injuring 2,500 others.
