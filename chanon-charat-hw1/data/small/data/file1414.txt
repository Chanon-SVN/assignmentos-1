==Events==
* [[428]] &amp;ndash; [[Nestorius]] becomes [[Ecumenical Patriarch of Constantinople|Patriarch of Constantinople]].
* [[837]] &amp;ndash; [[Halley's Comet]] makes its closest approach to Earth at a distance equal to 0.0342 [[Astronomical unit|AU]] (5.1 million kilometres/3.2 million miles).
* [[879]] &amp;ndash; [[Louis III of France|Louis III]] and [[Carloman II]] become joint [[List of French monarchs|Kings of the Western Franks]].
*[[1407]] &amp;ndash; The lama [[Deshin Shekpa]] visits the [[Ming Dynasty]] capital at [[Nanjing]]. He is awarded the title &quot;Great Treasure Prince of Dharma&quot;.
*[[1500]] &amp;ndash; [[Ludovico Sforza]] is captured by [[Switzerland|Swiss]] troops at [[Novara]] and is handed over to the French.
*[[1606]] &amp;ndash; The [[London Company|Virginia Company of London]] is established by royal charter by [[James I of England]] with the purpose of establishing [[British colonization of the Americas|colonial settlements in North America]].
*[[1710]] &amp;ndash; The [[Statute of Anne]], the first law regulating [[copyright]], comes into force in [[Kingdom of Great Britain|Great Britain]].
*[[1741]] &amp;ndash; [[War of the Austrian Succession]]: [[10 April 1755[1] – 2 July 1843]] defeats [[History of Austria#Charles VI and Maria Theresa (1711–1780)|Austria]] at the [[Battle of Mollwitz]].
*[[1755]] &amp;ndash; [[Samuel Hahnemann]] (10 April 1755 – 2 July 1843) was a German physician, best known for creating a system of alternative medicine called [[homeopathy]].
*[[1809]] &amp;ndash; [[Napoleonic Wars]]: The [[War of the Fifth Coalition]] begins when forces of the [[Austrian Empire]] invade [[Bavaria]].
*[[1815]] &amp;ndash; The [[Mount Tambora]] volcano begins a three-month-long eruption, lasting until [[July 15]]. The eruption ultimately kills 71,000 people and [[Year Without a Summer|affects Earth's climate]] for the next two years.
*[[1816]] &amp;ndash; The [[Federal government of the United States]] approves the creation of the [[Second Bank of the United States]].
*[[1821]] &amp;ndash; [[Patriarch Gregory V of Constantinople]] is hanged by the [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman government]] from the main gate of the [[Patriarchate]] and his body is thrown into the [[Bosphorus]].
*[[1826]] &amp;ndash; The 10,500 inhabitants of the Greek town of [[Missolonghi]] begin leaving the town after a year's siege by Turkish forces. Very few of them survive.
*[[1856]] &amp;ndash; The [[Theta Chi]] fraternity is founded at [[Norwich University]] in [[Vermont]].
*[[1858]] &amp;ndash; After the original [[Big Ben]], a {{Convert|14.5|tonne|lb}} bell for the [[Palace of Westminster]] had cracked during testing, it is recast into the current {{convert|13.76|tonne|lb}} bell by [[Whitechapel Bell Foundry]].
*[[1864]] &amp;ndash; Archduke [[maximilian I of Mexico|Maximilian of Habsburg]] is proclaimed emperor of Mexico during the [[French intervention in Mexico]].
*[[1865]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: A day after his surrender to [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] forces, [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] General [[Robert E. Lee]] [[Lee's Farewell Address|addresses]] his troops for the last time.
*[[1866]] &amp;ndash; The [[American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals]] (ASPCA) is founded in New York City by [[Henry Bergh]].
*[[1868]] &amp;ndash; At Arogee in [[Ethiopia|Abyssinia]], British and Indian forces [[British Expedition to Abyssinia|defeat]] an army of Emperor [[Tewodros II]]. While 700 [[Ethiopia]]ns are killed and many more injured, only two British/Indian troops die.
*[[1872]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Arbor Day]] is celebrated in [[Nebraska]].
*[[1887]] &amp;ndash; On [[Easter|Easter Sunday]], [[Pope Leo XIII]] authorizes the establishment of [[The Catholic University of America]].
*[[1904]] &amp;ndash; British mystic [[Aleister Crowley]] transcribes the third and final chapter of ''[[The Book of the Law]]''.
*[[1912]] &amp;ndash; ''[[RMS Titanic]]'' sets sail from [[Southampton]], England on her maiden and only voyage.
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; The [[Professional Golfers' Association of America]] (PGA) is created in New York City.
*[[1919]] &amp;ndash; [[Mexican Revolution]] leader [[Emiliano Zapata]] is ambushed and shot dead by [[government]] forces in [[Morelos]].
*[[1925]] &amp;ndash; ''[[The Great Gatsby]]'' by [[F. Scott Fitzgerald]] is first published in New York City, by [[Charles Scribner's Sons]].
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: The [[Axis powers]] in Europe establish the [[Independent State of Croatia]] from occupied [[Kingdom of Yugoslavia|Yugoslavia]] with [[Ante Pavelić]]'s [[Ustaše]] fascist [[Insurgency|insurgent]]s in power.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; [[Rudolf Vrba]] and [[Alfréd Wetzler]] escape from the [[Auschwitz concentration camp|Birkenau]] death camp.
*[[1953]] &amp;ndash; [[Warner Bros.]] premieres the first [[3-D film]] from a major American studio, entitled ''[[House of Wax (1953 film)|House of Wax]]''.
*[[1957]] &amp;ndash; The [[Suez Canal]] is reopened for all shipping after being closed for three months.
*[[1959]] &amp;ndash; [[Akihito]], future [[Emperor of Japan]], marries [[Empress Michiko of Japan|Michiko]].
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; One hundred twenty-nine American sailors die when the [[submarine]] {{USS|Thresher|SSN-593|6}} sinks at sea.
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; New Zealand inter-island ferry {{ship|TEV|Wahine}} founders and sinks at the mouth of [[Wellington Harbour]].
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; [[Paul McCartney]] announces that he is leaving [[The Beatles]] for personal and professional reasons.
*[[1971]] &amp;ndash; [[Ping-pong diplomacy]]: In an attempt to thaw relations with the United States, the People's Republic of China hosts the U.S. [[table tennis]] team for a week-long visit.
*[[1972]] &amp;ndash; Twenty days after he is kidnapped in [[Buenos Aires]], [[Oberdan Sallustro]] is murdered by [[communist]] guerrillas.
*  1972   &amp;ndash; [[Yinqueshan Han Tombs|Tombs]] containing [[Yinqueshan Han Slips|bamboo slips]], among them [[Sun Tzu]]'s ''[[The Art of War|Art of War]]'' and [[Sun Bin]]'s lost [[Sun Bin's Art of War|military treatise]], are accidentally discovered by construction workers in [[Shandong]].
*  1972   &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: For the first time since November [[1967]], American [[B-52 Stratofortress|B-52 bombers]] reportedly begin bombing [[North Vietnam]].
*  1972   &amp;ndash; Seventy-four nations sign the [[Biological Weapons Convention]], the first multilateral disarmament treaty banning the production of [[biological weapon]]s.
*[[1973]] &amp;ndash; A British [[Vickers Vanguard]] [[turboprop]] aircraft crashes in a snowstorm at [[Basel]], Switzerland killing 104 people.
*[[1979]] &amp;ndash; [[1979 Red River Valley tornado outbreak|Red River Valley tornado outbreak]]: A tornado lands in [[Wichita Falls, Texas]] killing 42 people.
*[[1988]] &amp;ndash; The [[Ojhri Camp]] disaster: Killing more than 1,000 people in [[Rawalpindi]] and [[Islamabad]] as a result of rockets and other munitions expelled by the blast.
*[[1991]] &amp;ndash; Italian ferry {{ship|MS|Moby Prince||6}} collides with an oil tanker in dense fog off [[Livorno]], Italy killing 140.
*  1991   &amp;ndash; A [[South Atlantic tropical cyclone|rare]] [[tropical cyclone|tropical storm]] develops in the South [[Atlantic Ocean]] near [[Angola]]; the first to be documented by [[weather satellite|satellites]].
*[[1998]] &amp;ndash; [[Northern Ireland]] peace deal reached ([[Good Friday Agreement]]).
*[[2009]] &amp;ndash; [[President of Fiji]] [[Ratu Josefa Iloilo]] announces he will suspend the [[Constitution of Fiji|constitution]] and assume all governance in the country, creating a [[2009 Fijian constitutional crisis|constitutional crisis]].
*[[2010]] &amp;ndash; [[Polish Air Force]] Tu-154M [[2010 Polish Air Force Tu-154 crash|crashes]] near [[Smolensk]], [[Russia]], killing 96 people, including Polish President [[Lech Kaczyński]] and dozens of other senior officials
*[[2014]] &amp;ndash; [[Kathleen Sebelius]] resigns as Secretary of the [[United States Department of Health and Human Services]], in light of fallout from the botched rollout of [[HealthCare.gov]].
