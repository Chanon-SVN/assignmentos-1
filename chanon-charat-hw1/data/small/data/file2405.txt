An ''n''-gonal '''bipyramid''' or '''dipyramid''' is a [[polyhedron]] formed by joining an ''n''-gonal [[Pyramid (geometry)|pyramid]] and its [[mirror image]] base-to-base.

The referenced ''n''-gon in the name of the bipyramids is not an external face but an internal one, existing on the primary symmetry plane which connects the two pyramid halves.

The [[face-transitive]] bipyramids are the [[dual polyhedra]] of the [[Prism (geometry)|uniform prisms]] and will generally have [[isosceles triangle]] faces.

A bipyramid can be [[Perspective projection|projected]] on a sphere or [[globe]] as ''n'' equally spaced lines of [[longitude]] going from [[Geographical pole|pole]] to pole, and [[Bisection|bisected]] by a line around the [[equator]].

Bipyramid [[face (geometry)|face]]s, projected as [[spherical triangle]]s, represent the fundamental domains in the [[List of spherical symmetry groups#Dihedral|dihedral symmetry]] D&lt;sub&gt;nh&lt;/sub&gt;.

==Volume==
The [[volume]] of a bipyramid is &lt;math&gt;\scriptstyle{V =} \tfrac{2}{3} \scriptstyle{Bh}&lt;/math&gt; where ''B'' is the area of the base and ''h'' the height from the base to the apex. This works for any location of the apex, provided that ''h'' is measured as the [[perpendicular]] distance from the [[plane (geometry)|plane]] which contains the base.

The volume of a bipyramid whose base is a regular ''n''-sided [[polygon]] with side length ''s'' and whose height is ''h'' is therefore: 
:&lt;math&gt;V = \frac{n}{6}hs^2 \cot\frac{\pi}{n}.&lt;/math&gt;

==Equilateral triangle bipyramids==
Only three kinds of bipyramids can have all edges of the same length (which implies that all faces are [[equilateral triangle]]s, and thus the bipyramid is a [[deltahedron]]): the [[Triangular dipyramid|triangular]], tetragonal, and [[Pentagonal dipyramid|pentagonal]] bipyramids. The tetragonal bipyramid with identical edges, or [[regular octahedron]], counts among the [[Platonic solid]]s, while the triangular and pentagonal bipyramids with identical edges count among the [[Johnson solid]]s (J12 and J13).

== Kalidescopic symmetry ==
If the base is regular and the line through the [[Apex (geometry)|apexes]] intersects the base at its center, the [[List of spherical symmetry groups|symmetry group]] of the ''n''-agonal bipyramid has [[Dihedral symmetry in three dimensions|dihedral symmetry]] D&lt;sub&gt;''n''h&lt;/sub&gt; of order 4''n'', except in the case of a regular octahedron, which has the larger [[octahedral symmetry]] group O&lt;sub&gt;h&lt;/sub&gt; of order 48, which has three versions of D&lt;sub&gt;4h&lt;/sub&gt; as subgroups. The [[Point groups in three dimensions#Rotation groups|rotation group]] is D&lt;sub&gt;''n''&lt;/sub&gt; of order 2''n'', except in the case of a regular octahedron, which has the larger symmetry group O of order 24, which has three versions of D&lt;sub&gt;4&lt;/sub&gt; as subgroups.

The digonal faces of a [[Spherical polyhedron|spherical]] ''2n''-bipyramid represents the fundamental domains of [[dihedral symmetry in three dimensions]]: D&lt;sub&gt;nh&lt;/sub&gt;, [n,2], (*n22), order ''4n''. The reflection domains can be shown as alternately colored triangles as mirror images.

==4-polytopes with bipyramid cells==

The [[Dual polyhedron#Dual polytopes and tessellations|dual]] of the [[Rectification (geometry)|rectification]] of each [[convex regular 4-polytope]]s is a [[cell-transitive]] [[4-polytope]] with bipyramidal cells.  In the following, the apex vertex of the bipyramid is A and an equator vertex is E.  The distance between adjacent vertices on the equator EE=1, the apex to equator edge is AE and the distance between the apices is AA.  The bipyramid 4-polytope will have V&lt;sub&gt;A&lt;/sub&gt; vertices where the apices of N&lt;sub&gt;A&lt;/sub&gt; bipyramids meet.  It will have V&lt;sub&gt;E&lt;/sub&gt; vertices where the type E vertices of N&lt;sub&gt;E&lt;/sub&gt; bipyramids meet.  N&lt;sub&gt;AE&lt;/sub&gt; bipyramids meet along each type AE edge. N&lt;sub&gt;EE&lt;/sub&gt; bipyramids meet along each type EE edge.  C&lt;sub&gt;AE&lt;/sub&gt; is the cosine of the dihedral angle along an AE edge.  C&lt;sub&gt;EE&lt;/sub&gt; is the cosine of the [[dihedral angle]] along an EE edge.  As cells must fit around an edge, 
N&lt;sub&gt;AA&lt;/sub&gt; cos&lt;sup&gt;−1&lt;/sup&gt;(C&lt;sub&gt;AA&lt;/sub&gt;) ≤ 2&amp;pi;, N&lt;sub&gt;AE&lt;/sub&gt; cos&lt;sup&gt;−1&lt;/sup&gt;(C&lt;sub&gt;AE&lt;/sub&gt;) ≤ 2&amp;pi;.

==Higher dimensions==
In general, a ''bipyramid'' can be seen as an ''n''-[[polytope]] constructed with a (''n''−1)-polytope in a [[hyperplane]] with two points in opposite directions, equal distance perpendicular from the hyperplane. If the (''n''−1)-polytope is a regular polytope, it will have identical [[Pyramid (geometry)|pyramids]] [[Facet (geometry)|facets]].   An example is the [[16-cell]], which is an octahedral bipyramid, and more generally an n-[[orthoplex#Higher_dimensions|orthoplex]] is an (n-1)-orthoplex bypyramid.
