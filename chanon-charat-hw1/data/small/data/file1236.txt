==Events==
*[[503 BC]] &amp;ndash; According to the [[Fasti Triumphales]], [[Roman consul]] [[Publius Postumius Tubertus]] celebrated an [[ovation]] for a military victory over the [[Sabines]].
* [[686]] &amp;ndash; Maya king [[Yuknoom Yich'aak K'ahk']] assumes the crown of [[Calakmul]].
*[[1043]] &amp;ndash; [[Edward the Confessor]] is crowned [[List of English monarchs|King of England]].
*[[1077]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Parliament]] of [[Friuli]] is created.
*[[1559]] &amp;ndash; The [[Peace of Cateau-Cambrésis]] treaty is signed, ending the [[Italian Wars]].
*[[1834]] &amp;ndash; The generals in the [[Greek War of Independence]] stand trial for [[treason]].
*[[1860]] &amp;ndash; The first successful [[United States]] [[Pony Express]] run from [[Saint Joseph, Missouri]], to [[Sacramento, California]], begins.
*[[1865]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] forces capture [[Richmond, Virginia]], the capital of the [[Confederate States of America]].
*[[1882]] &amp;ndash; [[American Old West]]: [[Jesse James]] is killed by [[Robert Ford (outlaw)|Robert Ford]].
*[[1885]] &amp;ndash; [[Gottlieb Daimler]] is granted a [[Germany|German]] [[patent]] for his [[engine]] design.
*[[1888]] &amp;ndash; The first of eleven [[Whitechapel murders|unsolved brutal murders]] of women committed in or near the impoverished [[Whitechapel]] district in the [[East End]] of [[London]], occurs.
*[[1895]] &amp;ndash; The trial in the [[libel]] case brought by [[Oscar Wilde]] begins, eventually resulting in his imprisonment on charges of [[homosexuality]].
*[[1922]] &amp;ndash; [[Joseph Stalin]] becomes the first [[General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union]].
*[[1929]] &amp;ndash; {{RMS|Queen Mary}} is ordered from [[John Brown &amp; Company Shipbuilding and Engineering]] by [[Cunard Line]].
*[[1933]] &amp;ndash; First flight over [[Mount Everest]], a British expedition, led by the [[Douglas Douglas-Hamilton, 14th Duke of Hamilton|Marquis of Clydesdale]], and funded by [[Lucy, Lady Houston]].
*[[1936]] &amp;ndash; [[Bruno Richard Hauptmann]] is executed for the kidnapping and death of [[Lindbergh kidnapping|Charles Augustus Lindbergh, Jr.]], the baby son of pilot [[Charles Lindbergh]].
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Japan]]ese forces begin an assault on the [[United States]] and [[Philippines|Filipino]] troops on the [[Battle of Bataan|Bataan Peninsula]].
*[[1946]] &amp;ndash; Japanese [[Lt. General]] [[Masaharu Homma]] is executed in the Philippines for leading the [[Bataan Death March]].
*[[1948]] &amp;ndash; [[President of the United States|President]] [[Harry S. Truman]] signs the [[Marshall Plan]], authorizing $5&amp;nbsp;billion in aid for 16 countries.
*  1948   &amp;ndash; In [[Jeju-do|Jeju]], [[South Korea]], a civil-war-like period of violence and [[human rights abuses]] begins, known as the [[Jeju massacre]].
*[[1955]] &amp;ndash; The [[American Civil Liberties Union]] announces it will defend [[Allen Ginsberg]]'s book ''[[Howl]]'' against [[obscenity]] charges.
*[[1956]] &amp;ndash; [[Hudsonville-Standale Tornado of April 1956|Hudsonville-Standale Tornado]]: The western half of the [[Lower Peninsula of Michigan]] is struck by a deadly [[Fujita scale|F5]] tornado.
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; The [[Leadbeater's Possum]] is rediscovered in Australia after 72 years.
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; [[Martin Luther King, Jr.]] delivers his &quot;[[I've Been to the Mountaintop]]&quot; speech.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[United States Secretary of Defense]] [[Melvin Laird]] announces that the United States will start to &quot;[[Vietnamization|Vietnamize]]&quot; the war effort.
*[[1973]] &amp;ndash; [[Martin Cooper (inventor)|Martin Cooper]] of [[Motorola]] makes the [[History of mobile phones|first handheld mobile phone call]] to [[Joel S. Engel]] of [[Bell Labs]], though it took ten years for the [[DynaTAC 8000X]] to become the first such phone to be commercially released.
*[[1974]] &amp;ndash; [[Super Outbreak|The Super Outbreak]] occurs, the second biggest tornado outbreak in recorded history (after the [[April 25–28, 2011 tornado outbreak]]). The death toll is 315, with nearly 5,500 injured.
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; [[Bobby Fischer]] refuses to play in a [[chess]] match against [[Anatoly Karpov]], giving Karpov the title of World Champion by default.
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; The [[Osborne 1]], the first successful [[portable computer]], is unveiled at the [[West Coast Computer Faire]] in [[San Francisco]].
*[[1996]] &amp;ndash; Suspected &quot;Unabomber&quot; [[Theodore Kaczynski]] is captured at his cabin in [[Montana]], [[United States]].
*  1996   &amp;ndash; A [[United States Air Force]] airplane carrying [[United States Secretary of Commerce]] [[Ron Brown (U.S. politician)|Ron Brown]] [[1996 Croatia USAF CT-43 crash|crashes]] in [[Croatia]], killing all 35 on board.
*[[1997]] &amp;ndash; The [[Thalit massacre]] begins in [[Algeria]]; all but one of the 53 inhabitants of [[Thalit]] are killed by guerrillas.
*[[2000]] &amp;ndash; ''[[United States v. Microsoft]]'': [[Microsoft]] is ruled to have violated [[United States antitrust laws]] by keeping &quot;an oppressive thumb&quot; on its competitors.
*[[2004]] &amp;ndash; [[Islamic]] [[terrorists]] involved in the [[11 March 2004 Madrid attacks]] are trapped by the police in their apartment and kill themselves.
*[[2007]] &amp;ndash; [[TGV world speed record#Record of 2007|Conventional-Train World Speed Record]]: a French [[TGV]] train on the [[LGV Est]] high speed line sets an official new world speed record.
*[[2008]] &amp;ndash; [[ATA Airlines]], once one of the ten largest U.S. passenger airlines and largest charter airline, files for bankruptcy for the second time in five years and ceases all operations.
*  2008   &amp;ndash; [[Texas]] law enforcement cordons off the [[Fundamentalist Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints|FLDS]]'s [[YFZ Ranch]]. Eventually 533 women and children will be removed and taken into state custody.
*[[2009]] &amp;ndash; Jiverly Antares Wong [[Binghamton shootings|opens fire]] at an [[American Civic Association]] immigration center in [[Binghamton]], [[New York|NY]], killing thirteen and wounding four before committing suicide.
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; More than 50 people die in [[2013 Argentina floods|floods]] resulting from record-breaking rainfall in [[La Plata]] and [[Buenos Aires]], Argentina.
