==Events==
*[[753 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Romulus]] founds [[Rome]] ([[founding of Rome|traditional date]]).
*[[43 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Mutina]]: [[Mark Antony]] is again defeated in battle by [[Aulus Hirtius]], who is killed. Antony fails to capture [[Mutina]] and [[Decimus Junius Brutus Albinus|Decimus Brutus]] is murdered shortly after.
*[[900]] &amp;ndash; The [[Laguna Copperplate Inscription]], the earliest known written document found in the [[Philippines]]: the Honourable Namwaran and his children, Lady Angkatan and Bukah, are granted pardon from all their debts by the Commander in chief of [[Kingdom of Tondo|Tundun]], as represented by the Honourable Jayadewa, Lord Minister of [[Pila, Laguna|Pailah]]. [[Luzon]], [[Philippines]].
*[[1092]] &amp;ndash; The [[Diocese of Pisa]] is elevated to the rank of metropolitan [[archdiocese]] by [[Pope Urban II]]
*[[1506]] &amp;ndash; The three-day [[Lisbon Massacre]] comes to an end with the slaughter of over 1,900 suspected Jews by Portuguese Catholics.
*[[1509]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry VIII of England|Henry VIII]] ascends the throne of England on the death of his father, [[Henry VII of England|Henry VII]].
*[[1526]] &amp;ndash; The last ruler of the [[Lodi Dynasty]], [[Ibrahim Lodi]] is defeated and killed by [[Babur]] in the [[Battle of Panipat (1526)|First Battle of Panipat]].
*[[1615]] &amp;ndash; The [[Wignacourt Aqueduct]] is inaugurated in [[History of Malta under the Order of Saint John|Malta]].
*[[1782]] &amp;ndash; The city of Rattanakosin, now known internationally as [[Bangkok]], is [[History of Bangkok|founded]] on the eastern bank of the Chao Phraya River by King [[Buddha Yodfa Chulaloke]].
*[[1792]] &amp;ndash; [[Tiradentes]], a [[revolutionary]] leading a movement for [[Brazilian independence|Brazil's independence]], is [[hanged, drawn and quartered]].
*[[1806]] &amp;ndash; [[Action of 21 April 1806]]: A French frigate escapes British forces off the coast of South Africa.
*[[1809]] &amp;ndash; Two [[Austria]]n army corps are driven from [[Landshut]] by a [[First French Empire]] army led by [[Napoleon I of France]] as two French corps to the north hold off the main Austrian army on the first day of the [[Battle of Eckmühl]].
*[[1821]] &amp;ndash; [[Benderli Ali Pasha]] arrives in Constantinople as the new [[Grand Vizier of the Ottoman Empire]]; he remains in power for only nine days before being sent into exile.&lt;ref&gt;İsmail Hâmi Danişmend, ''Osmanlı Devlet Erkânı'', Türkiye Yayınevi, İstanbul, 1971, p. 72. {{Tr icon}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*[[1836]] &amp;ndash; [[Texas Revolution]]: The [[Battle of San Jacinto]]: [[Republic of Texas]] forces under [[Sam Houston]] defeat troops under [[Mexico|Mexican]] [[General]] [[Antonio López de Santa Anna]].
*[[1856]] &amp;ndash; [[Australian labour movement]]: Stonemasons and building workers on building sites around Melbourne march from the University of Melbourne to Parliament House to achieve an [[Eight-hour day]].
*[[1863]] &amp;ndash; [[Bahá'u'lláh]], the founder of the [[Bahá'í Faith]], declares his mission as &quot;[[He whom God shall make manifest]]&quot;.
*[[1894]] &amp;ndash; [[Norway]] formally adopts the [[Krag-Jørgensen]] bolt-action rifle as the main arm of its armed forces, a weapon that would remain in service for almost 50 years.
*[[1898]] &amp;ndash; [[Spanish–American War]]: The [[United States Navy]] begins a [[blockade]] of [[Cuba]]n ports. When the [[United States Congress|U.S. Congress]] issued a [[declaration of war]] on April 25, it declared that a state of war had existed from this date.
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[Ypiranga incident]]: A German arms shipment to Mexico is intercepted by the U.S. Navy near [[Veracruz, Veracruz]].
*[[1918]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: [[German Empire|German]] fighter ace [[Manfred von Richthofen]], better known as &quot;[[The Red Baron]]&quot;, is shot down and killed over [[Vaux-sur-Somme]] in [[French Third Republic|France]].
*[[1925]] &amp;ndash; The [[Manifesto of the Fascist Intellectuals]] is published in ''Il Mondo'', establishing the political and ideological foundations of [[Italian Fascism]].&lt;ref&gt;Jeffrey T. Schnapp (1996). [http://www.jstor.org/stable/261164 Fascinating Fascism]. ''Journal of Contemporary History'', Vol. 31, No. 2, Special Issue: The Aesthetics of Fascism (Apr., 1996), pp. 235-244, accessed 4 February 2009.&lt;/ref&gt; 
*[[1934]] &amp;ndash; The &quot;Surgeon's Photograph&quot;, the most famous photo allegedly showing the [[Loch Ness Monster]], is published in the ''[[Daily Mail]]'' (in 1999, it is revealed to be a hoax).
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; [[Emmanouil Tsouderos]] becomes the 132nd [[Prime Minister of Greece]].
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] forces south of [[Berlin]] at [[Zossen]] attack the [[Nazi Germany|German]] [[Oberkommando der Wehrmacht|High Command]] headquarters.
*[[1952]] &amp;ndash; [[Secretary's Day]] (now Administrative Professionals' Day) is first celebrated.
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; [[Brasília]], [[Brazil]]'s [[capital (political)|capital]], is officially inaugurated.  At 09:30, the Three Powers of the Republic are simultaneously transferred from the old capital, [[Rio de Janeiro]].
*[[1962]] &amp;ndash; The [[Century 21 Exposition|Seattle World's Fair]] (Century 21 Exposition) opens. It is the first [[World's Fair]] in the United States since World War II.
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; The [[Universal House of Justice]] of the Bahá'í Faith is elected for the first time.
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; A [[Transit (satellite)|Transit-5bn]] satellite fails to reach orbit after launch; as it re-enters the atmosphere, {{convert|2.1|lb}} of radioactive [[plutonium]] in its [[Systems Nuclear Auxiliary Power Program|SNAP]] [[Radioisotope thermoelectric generator|RTG]] power source is widely dispersed.
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; The [[1964 New York World's Fair|1964–1965 New York World's Fair]] opens for its second and final season.
*[[1966]] &amp;ndash; [[Rastafari movement]]: [[Haile Selassie]] of [[Ethiopia]] visits [[Jamaica]], an event now celebrated as [[Grounation Day]].
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; [[Greek military junta of 1967–74]]: A few days before the [[general election]] in [[Greece]], [[Colonel]] [[George Papadopoulos]] leads a [[coup d'état]], establishing a [[Greek military junta of 1967–74|military regime]] that lasts for seven years.
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; The [[Hutt River Province Principality]] secedes from [[Australia]].
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[President]] of [[South Vietnam]] [[Nguyen Van Thieu]] flees [[Ho Chi Minh City|Saigon]], as [[Xuan Loc]], the last South Vietnamese outpost blocking a direct [[North Vietnam]]ese assault on Saigon, falls.
*[[1982]] &amp;ndash; [[Baseball]]: [[Rollie Fingers]] of the [[Milwaukee Brewers]] becomes the first [[pitcher]] to record 300 saves.
*[[1987]] &amp;ndash; The [[Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam|Tamil Tigers]] are blamed for a [[car bomb]] that detonates in the [[Sri Lanka]]n capital city of [[Colombo]], killing 106 people.
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; [[Tiananmen Square Protests of 1989]]: In [[Beijing]], around 100,000 students gather in [[Tiananmen Square]] to commemorate [[China|Chinese]] reform leader [[Hu Yaobang]].
*[[1992]] &amp;ndash; The first discoveries of [[extrasolar planet]]s are announced by [[astronomers]] [[Alexander Wolszczan]] and [[Dale Frail]].  They discovered two planets orbiting the pulsar PSR 1257+12.
*[[1993]] &amp;ndash; The Supreme Court in [[La Paz]], [[Bolivia]], sentences former dictator [[Luis Garcia Meza]] to 30 years in jail without parole for murder, theft, fraud and violating the constitution.
*[[2004]] &amp;ndash; Five suicide car bombers [[21 April 2004 Basra bombings|target police stations]] in and around [[Basra]], killing 74 people and wounding 160.
*[[2010]] &amp;ndash; The controversial [[2010 Kharkiv Pact|Kharkiv Pact]] (Russian Ukrainian Naval Base for Gas Treaty) is signed in [[Kharkiv]], Ukraine, by [[Ukrainian President]] [[Viktor Yanukovych]] and [[Russian President]] [[Dimitry Medvedev]]; it will be unilaterally terminated by Russia on March 31, 2014.
*[[2012]] &amp;ndash; Two trains are involved in a [[Sloterdijk train collision|head-on collision]] near [[Sloterdijk, Amsterdam]], in the [[Netherlands]], injuring 116 people.
