'''Bill Macy''' (born '''Wolf Martin Garber'''; May 18, 1922) is an [[United States|American]] television, film and stage actor, born in [[Revere, Massachusetts]], to Mollie (n√©e Friedopfer) and Michael Garber, a manufacturer.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/93/Bill-Macy.html|title=Bill Macy Biography (1922-)|publisher=|accessdate=25 December 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;  He was raised in [[Brooklyn, New York]] and worked as a cab driver before pursuing acting.

Macy is best known for playing Walter Findlay, the long-suffering husband of the title character on the 1970s [[television]] [[situation comedy]] ''[[Maude (TV series)|Maude]]'', played by [[Beatrice Arthur]]. He was also an original cast member of the long-running theatrical revue ''[[Oh! Calcutta!]]'' He has made more than 70 appearances on film and television, including a memorable role as the co-inventor of the 'Opti-grab' in the 1979 [[Steve Martin]] comedy ''[[The Jerk]]'', and as the head television writer in ''[[My Favorite Year]]'' (1982).

He appeared in the popular television movie ''Perry Mason &amp; The Case Of The Murdered Madame'' (1987) as banker Richard Wilson, who, along with three other bankers, employed the murder victim's public relation company prior to her murder. 

He appeared occasionally on ''[[Seinfeld]]'' as one of the residents of the Florida retirement community in which [[Jerry Seinfeld]]'s parents lived. He also appeared on the short-lived sitcom ''[[Back to You (TV series)|Back to You]]''.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0534409/|title=Bill Macy|work=IMDb|accessdate=25 December 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; He made a guest appearance as a patient on ''[[Chicago Hope]]'' and as an aging gambler on the series ''[[Las Vegas (TV series)|Las Vegas]]''. In 2006 he made an appearance on ''[[My Name is Earl]]'' in the second-season episode, &quot;[[Van Hickey]]&quot; as an elderly patient in a nursing home who claims he &quot;once tongue-kissed a Jamaican woman.&quot;

Macy is only five days younger than actress [[Bea Arthur]], his co-star in ''[[Maude (TV series)|Maude]]''.
