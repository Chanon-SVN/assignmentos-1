==Events==
* [[397]] &amp;ndash; The wearing of barbarian clothing in the City of Rome is banned by the Emperor [[Honorius (emperor)|Honorius]].
* [[451]] &amp;ndash; [[Attila|Attila the Hun]] sacks the town of [[Metz]] and attacks other cities in [[Gaul]].
* [[529]] &amp;ndash; First draft of the ''[[Corpus Juris Civilis]]'' (a fundamental work in [[jurisprudence]]) is issued by [[Eastern Roman Emperor]] [[Justinian I]].
* [[611]] &amp;ndash; Maya king [[Scroll Serpent|Uneh Chan]] of [[Calakmul]] sacks rival city-state [[Palenque]] in southern Mexico.
*[[1141]] &amp;ndash; [[Empress Matilda]], became the first female ruler of [[England]], adopting the title 'Lady of the English'
*[[1348]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles University in Prague|Charles University]] is founded in [[Prague]].
*[[1521]] &amp;ndash; [[Ferdinand Magellan]] arrives at [[Cebu (island)|Cebu]].
*[[1541]] &amp;ndash; [[Francis Xavier]] leaves [[Lisbon]] on a mission to the [[Portugal|Portuguese]] [[East Indies]].
*[[1724]] &amp;ndash; Premiere performance of [[Johann Sebastian Bach]]'s ''[[St John Passion]]'' [[Bach-Werke-Verzeichnis|BWV]] 245 at [[St. Nicholas Church, Leipzig]].
*[[1767]] &amp;ndash; End of [[Burmese–Siamese War (1765–1767)]]
*[[1776]] &amp;ndash; [[John Barry (naval officer)|Captain John Barry]] and the {{USS|Lexington|1776|6}} captures the ''Edward''.
*[[1788]] &amp;ndash; [[American Pioneers to the Northwest Territory]] arrive at the confluence of the [[Ohio River|Ohio]] and [[Muskingum River|Muskingum]] rivers, establishing [[Marietta, Ohio]], as the first permanent American settlement of the new [[United States]] in the [[Northwest Territory]], and opening the westward expansion of the new country.
*[[1789]] &amp;ndash; [[Selim III]] became [[Sultan of the Ottoman Empire]] and [[Caliph of Islam]].
*[[1798]] &amp;ndash; The [[Mississippi Territory]] is organized from disputed territory claimed by both the [[United States]] and [[Spain]].  It is expanded in 1804 and again in 1812.
*[[1805]] &amp;ndash; [[Lewis and Clark Expedition]]: The [[Corps of Discovery]] breaks camp among the [[Mandan]] tribe and resumes its journey [[West]] along the [[Missouri River]].
*[[1827]] &amp;ndash; [[John Walker (inventor)|John Walker]], an [[English people|English]] [[pharmacist|chemist]], sells the first [[match|friction match]] that he had invented the previous year.
*[[1829]] &amp;ndash; [[Joseph Smith, Jr.]], founder of the [[Latter Day Saint movement]], commences translation of the [[Book of Mormon]], with [[Oliver Cowdery]] as his scribe.
*[[1831]] &amp;ndash; [[D. Pedro I]], Emperor of [[Brazil]], resigns. He goes to his native [[Portugal]] to become King D. Pedro IV.
*[[1862]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Battle of Shiloh]] ends &amp;ndash; the [[Union Army]] under [[General]] [[Ulysses S. Grant]] defeats the [[Confederate States of America|Confederates]] near [[Shiloh National Military Park|Shiloh, Tennessee]].
*[[1868]] &amp;ndash; [[Thomas D'Arcy McGee]], one of the Canadian [[Fathers of Confederation]] is assassinated by [[Irish republicanism|Irish Republicans]], in one of the few Canadian political assassinations, and the only one of a federal politician.
*[[1890]] &amp;ndash; Completion of the first [[Lake Biwa Canal]].
*[[1906]] &amp;ndash; [[Mount Vesuvius]] erupts and devastates [[Naples]].
*[[1906]] &amp;ndash; The [[Algeciras Conference]] gives [[France]] and [[Spain]] control over [[Morocco]].
*[[1908]] &amp;ndash; [[H. H. Asquith]] of the [[Liberal Party (UK)|Liberal Party]] takes office as [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]], succeeding Sir [[Henry Campbell-Bannerman]]
*[[1922]] &amp;ndash; [[Teapot Dome scandal]]: [[United States Secretary of the Interior]] leases [[Teapot Dome]] [[petroleum]] reserves in [[Wyoming]].
*[[1927]] &amp;ndash; First long-distance [[public television]] broadcast (from [[Washington, D.C.]], to [[New York City]], displaying the image of [[Commerce Secretary]] [[Herbert Hoover]]).
*[[1933]] &amp;ndash; [[Prohibition]] in the United States is repealed for [[beer]] of no more than 3.2% [[alcohol]] by weight, eight months before the ratification of the [[Twenty-first Amendment to the United States Constitution|XXI amendment]].
*[[1939]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Italy]] invades [[Albania]].
*[[1940]] &amp;ndash; [[Booker T. Washington]] becomes the first [[African American]] to be depicted on a [[United States]] [[postage stamp]].
*[[1943]] &amp;ndash; [[The Holocaust|Holocaust]]: In Terebovlia, Ukraine, [[Germans]] order 1,100 [[Jews]] to undress to their underwear and march through the city of [[Terebovlia]] to the nearby village of Plebanivka where they are shot dead and buried in ditches.
*  1943   &amp;ndash; [[Ioannis Rallis]] becomes collaborationist [[Prime Minister of Greece]] during the [[Axis occupation of Greece|Axis Occupation]].
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; World War II: The [[Japanese battleship Yamato|Japanese battleship ''Yamato'']], the largest battleship ever constructed, is sunk by American planes 200 miles north of [[Okinawa]] while en route to a [[suicide mission]] in [[Operation Ten-Go]].
*  1945   &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Visoko, Bosnia and Herzegovina|Visoko]] is liberated by the 7th, 9th, and 17th Krajina brigades from the Tenth division of [[Yugoslav Partisan]] forces.
*[[1946]] &amp;ndash; [[Syria]]'s independence from [[France]] is officially recognised.
*[[1948]] &amp;ndash; The [[World Health Organization]] is established by the [[United Nations]].
*  1948   &amp;ndash; A [[Buddhism|Buddhist]] monastery burns in [[Shanghai]], [[China]], leaving twenty monks dead.
*[[1954]] &amp;ndash; [[President of the United States|President]] [[Dwight D. Eisenhower]] gives his &quot;[[domino theory]]&quot; speech during a news conference.
*[[1955]] &amp;ndash; [[Winston Churchill]] resigns as [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]] amid indications of failing health.
*[[1956]] &amp;ndash; [[Spain]] relinquishes its [[Spanish Morocco|protectorate in Morocco]].
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; [[International Business Machines|IBM]] announces the [[IBM System/360|System/360]].
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; Film critic [[Roger Ebert]] published his very first film review in the ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]''.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; The [[Internet]]'s symbolic birth date: publication of [[Request for Comments|RFC]] 1.
*[[1971]] &amp;ndash; President [[Richard Milhous Nixon|Richard Nixon]] announces his decision to increase the rate of American troop withdrawals from [[Vietnam]].
*[[1976]] &amp;ndash; Former British Cabinet Minister [[John Stonehouse]] resigns from the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]].
*[[1977]] &amp;ndash; [[Germany|German]] Federal prosecutor [[Siegfried Buback]] and his driver are shot by two [[Red Army Faction]] members while waiting at a red light.
*[[1978]] &amp;ndash; Development of the [[neutron bomb]] is canceled by President [[Jimmy Carter]].
*[[1980]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States]] severs relations with the [[Iran|Islamic Republic of Iran]].
*[[1983]] &amp;ndash; During [[STS-6]], astronauts [[Story Musgrave]] and [[Donald H. Peterson|Don Peterson]] perform the first [[space shuttle]] spacewalk.
*[[1985]] &amp;ndash; Soviet leader [[Mikhail Gorbachev]] declares a moratorium on the deployment of middle-range missiles in [[Europe]].
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; [[Soviet submarine K-278 Komsomolets|Soviet submarine Komsomolets]] sinks in the [[Barents Sea]] off the coast of [[Norway]] killing 42 sailors.
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; [[Iran Contra Affair]]: [[John Poindexter]] is found guilty of five charges for his part in the scandal (the conviction is later reversed on appeal).
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; A fire breaks out on the [[passenger ferry]] [[M/S Scandinavian Star|M/S ''Scandinavian Star'']], killing 158 people.
*[[1992]] &amp;ndash; [[Republika Srpska]] announces its independence.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; [[Rwandan Genocide]]: Massacres of [[Tutsis]] begin in [[Kigali]], [[Rwanda]].
*  1994   &amp;ndash; Auburn Calloway attempts to [[Aircraft hijacking|hijack]] [[FedEx Express Flight 705]] and crash it to allow his family to benefit from his [[life insurance]] policy. The crew subdues him and lands the aircraft safely.
*[[1995]] &amp;ndash; [[First Chechen War]]: [[Russia]]n [[paramilitary]] troops begin a [[Samashki massacre|massacre]] of civilians in [[Samashki]], [[Chechnya]].
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; The [[World Trade Organization]] rules in favor of the [[United States]] in its long-running trade dispute with the [[European Union]] over bananas.
*[[2001]] &amp;ndash; [[Mars Odyssey]] is launched.
*[[2003]] &amp;ndash; U.S. troops capture [[Baghdad]]; [[Saddam Hussein]]'s regime falls two days later.
*[[2009]] &amp;ndash; Former [[Peruvian]] President [[Alberto Fujimori]] is sentenced to 25 years in prison for ordering killings and kidnappings by security forces.
*  2009   &amp;ndash; [[2009 Moldova civil unrest|Mass protests]] begin across [[Moldova]] under the belief that results from the [[Moldovan parliamentary election, April 2009|parliamentary election]] are fraudulent.
