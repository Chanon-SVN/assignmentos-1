==Events==
* [[475]] &amp;ndash; The [[Roman Empire|Roman]] [[general]] [[Orestes (Roman soldier)|Orestes]] forces western [[Roman Emperors|Roman Emperor]] [[Julius Nepos]] to flee his [[capital city]], [[Ravenna]].
* [[489]] &amp;ndash; [[Theodoric the Great|Theodoric]], king of the [[Ostrogoths]] defeats [[Odoacer]] at the [[Battle of Isonzo (489)|Battle of Isonzo]], forcing his way into [[Italy]].
* [[663]] &amp;ndash; [[Silla]]–[[Tang Dynasty|Tang]] armies crush the [[Baekje]] restoration attempt and force [[Yamato period|Yamato Japan]] to withdraw from Korea in the [[Battle of Baekgang]].
*[[1189]] &amp;ndash; [[Third Crusade]]: The Crusaders begin the [[Siege of Acre (1189–1191)|Siege of Acre]] under [[Guy of Lusignan]].
*[[1521]] &amp;ndash; The [[Ottoman Turks]] occupy [[Belgrade]].
*[[1524]] &amp;ndash; The [[Kaqchikel people|Kaqchikel]] [[Maya civilization|Maya]] rebel against their former Spanish allies during the [[Spanish conquest of Guatemala]].
*[[1542]] &amp;ndash; [[Ottoman–Portuguese conflicts (1538–57)|Turkish–Portuguese War (1538–1557)]]: [[Battle of Wofla]]: The Portuguese are scattered, their leader [[Christovão da Gama]] is captured and later executed.
*[[1565]] &amp;ndash; [[Pedro Menéndez de Avilés]] sights land near [[St. Augustine, Florida]] and founds the oldest continuously occupied European-established city in the [[continental United States]].
*[[1609]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry Hudson]] discovers [[Delaware Bay]].
*[[1619]] &amp;ndash; [[Ferdinand II of Germany|Ferdinand II]] is elected [[emperor]] of the [[Holy Roman Empire]].
*[[1640]] &amp;ndash; [[Bishops' Wars#Second Bishops' War (1640)|Second Bishop's War]]: King [[Charles I of England|Charles I]]'s English army loses to a [[Covenanter|Scottish Covenanter]] force at the [[Battle of Newburn]].
*[[1648]] &amp;ndash; [[Siege of Colchester]] ended when Royalists Forces surrender to the Parliamentary Forces after eleven weeks, during the [[English Civil War]].
*[[1709]] &amp;ndash; [[Pamheiba|Meidingnu Pamheiba]] is crowned King of [[Manipur]].
*[[1789]] &amp;ndash; [[William Herschel]] discovers a new moon of Saturn: [[Enceladus]].
*[[1810]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Grand Port]]: The French accept the surrender of a [[British Navy]] fleet.
*[[1830]] &amp;ndash; The [[Baltimore and Ohio Railroad]]'s new ''[[Tom Thumb (locomotive)|Tom Thumb]]'' steam locomotive races a horse-drawn car, presaging steam's role in US railroads.
*[[1833]] &amp;ndash; The [[Slavery Abolition Act 1833]] receives [[Royal Assent]], abolishing slavery through most the British Empire.
*[[1845]] &amp;ndash; The first issue of ''[[Scientific American]]'' magazine is published.
*[[1849]] &amp;ndash; After a month-long siege, [[Venice]], which had declared itself independent as the [[Republic of San Marco]], surrenders to [[Austria]].
*[[1859]] &amp;ndash; The [[Carrington event]] disrupts [[electrical telegraph]] services and causes [[aurora]] to shine so brightly that they are seen clearly over the Earth's [[middle latitudes]].
*[[1861]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] forces attack [[Cape Hatteras]], [[North Carolina]] in the [[Battle of Hatteras Inlet Batteries]] which lasts for two days.
*[[1862]] &amp;ndash; American Civil War: [[Second Battle of Bull Run]], also known as the Battle of Second Manassas.  The battle ends on August 30.  
*[[1867]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States]] takes possession of the (at this point unoccupied) [[Midway Atoll]].
*[[1879]] &amp;ndash; [[Cetshwayo]], last king of the [[Zulu people|Zulu]]s, is captured by the [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|British]].
*[[1898]] &amp;ndash; [[Caleb Bradham]] invents the carbonated soft drink that will later be called &quot;[[Pepsi-Cola]]&quot;.
*[[1901]] &amp;ndash; [[Silliman University]] is founded in the [[Philippines]]. The first [[United States|American]] [[private school]] in the country.
*[[1909]] &amp;ndash; A group of mid-level [[Greek Army]] officers launches the [[Goudi coup]], seeking wide-ranging reforms.
*[[1913]] &amp;ndash; [[Wilhelmina of the Netherlands|Queen Wilhelmina]] opens the [[Peace Palace]] in [[The Hague]].
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: The [[Royal Navy]] defeats the [[German Empire|German]] fleet in the [[Battle of Heligoland Bight (1914)|Battle of Heligoland Bight]].
*  1914   &amp;ndash; World War I: German troops take the city of [[Namur (city)|Namur]] in [[Belgium]].
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; World War I: Germany declares war on [[Kingdom of Romania|Romania]].
*  1916   &amp;ndash; World War I: [[Kingdom of Italy|Italy]] declares war on Germany.
*[[1917]] &amp;ndash; Ten [[Suffragettes]] are arrested while picketing the [[White House]].
*[[1924]] &amp;ndash; The [[Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic|Georgian]] opposition stages the [[August Uprising]] against the [[Soviet Union]].
*[[1931]] &amp;ndash; [[France]] and the [[Soviet Union]] sign a treaty of non-aggression.
*[[1937]] &amp;ndash; [[Toyota Motors]] becomes an independent company.
*[[1943]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: In [[Denmark]], a [[general strike]] against the [[Nazism|Nazi]] occupation starts.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Marseille]] and [[Toulon]] are liberated.
*[[1953]] &amp;ndash; [[Nippon Television]] broadcasts [[Japan]]'s first television show, including its first TV advertisement.
*[[1955]] &amp;ndash; [[African American|Black]] teenager [[Emmett Till]] is brutally murdered in [[Mississippi]], galvanizing the nascent [[American Civil Rights Movement]].
*[[1957]] &amp;ndash; [[United States Senate|U.S. Senator]] [[Strom Thurmond]] begins a [[filibuster]] to prevent the Senate from voting on [[Civil Rights Act of 1957]]; he stopped speaking 24 hours and 18 minutes later, the longest filibuster ever conducted by a single Senator.
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; [[March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom]]: The Reverend [[Martin Luther King, Jr.]] gives his ''[[I Have a Dream]]'' speech
*  1963   &amp;ndash; [[Career Girls Murders|Emily Hoffert and Janice Wylie]] are murdered in their [[Upper East Side|Manhattan]] apartment, prompting the events that would lead to the passing of the [[Miranda warning|Miranda Rights]].
*  1963   &amp;ndash; The [[Governor Albert D. Rosellini Bridge—Evergreen Point|Evergreen Point Bridge]], the longest [[pontoon bridge|floating bridge]] in the world, opens between [[Seattle]] and [[Medina, Washington]], US.
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; The [[1964 Philadelphia race riot|Philadelphia race riot]] begins.
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; [[Riot]]s in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]], during the [[1968 Democratic National Convention|Democratic National Convention]].
*[[1979]] &amp;ndash; An [[Provisional Irish Republican Army|IRA]] bomb explodes at the [[Grote Markt (Brussels)|Grote Markt]] in [[Brussels]].
*[[1988]] &amp;ndash; [[Ramstein airshow disaster]]: Three aircraft of the [[Frecce Tricolori]] demonstration team collide and the wreckage falls into the crowd. Seventy-five are killed and 346 seriously injured.
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; [[Iraq]] declares [[Kuwait]] to be its newest [[Provinces of Iraq|province]].
*  1990   &amp;ndash; [[1990 Plainfield tornado|An F5 tornado]] strikes the [[Illinois]] cities of [[Plainfield, Illinois|Plainfield]] and [[Joliet, Illinois|Joliet]], killing 29 people.
*[[1996]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles, Prince of Wales]] and [[Diana, Princess of Wales]] divorce.
*[[1998]] &amp;ndash; [[Pakistan]]'s [[National Assembly of Pakistan|National Assembly]] passes a [[Constitution of Pakistan|constitutional]] amendment to make the &quot;[[Qur'an]] and [[Sunnah]]&quot; the &quot;supreme law&quot; but the bill is defeated in the [[Senate of Pakistan|Senate]].
*  1998   &amp;ndash; [[Second Congo War]]: [[Armed Forces of the Democratic Republic of the Congo|Loyalist troops]] backed by [[Angola]]n and [[Zimbabwean]] forces repulse the [[Rally for Congolese Democracy|RCD]] and [[Rwanda]]n offensive on [[Kinshasa]].
*[[2003]] &amp;ndash; An [[2003 London blackout|electricity blackout]] cuts off power to around 500,000 people living in south east England and brings 60% of London's underground rail network to a halt.
