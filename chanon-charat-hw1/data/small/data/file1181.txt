==Events==
*[[1303]] &amp;ndash; The [[Sapienza University of Rome]] is instituted by [[Pope Boniface VIII]].
*[[1453]] &amp;ndash; The last naval battle in [[Byzantine Empire|Byzantine]] history occurs, as three Genoese galleys escorting a Byzantine transport fight their way through the huge Ottoman blockade fleet and into the [[Golden Horn]].
*[[1534]] &amp;ndash; [[Jacques Cartier]] begins the voyage during which he discovers Canada and [[Labrador]].
*[[1535]] &amp;ndash; The [[Sun dog]] phenomenon observed over [[Stockholm]] and depicted in the famous painting ''[[Vädersolstavlan]]''.
*[[1653]] &amp;ndash; [[Oliver Cromwell]] dissolves the [[Rump Parliament]].
*[[1657]] &amp;ndash; Admiral [[Robert Blake (admiral)|Robert Blake]] destroys a Spanish silver fleet under heavy fire at the [[Battle of Santa Cruz de Tenerife (1657)|Battle of Santa Cruz de Tenerife]].
*  1657   &amp;ndash; [[Freedom of religion]] is granted to the [[Jew]]s of [[New Amsterdam]] (later New York City).
*[[1689]] &amp;ndash; The former king, [[James II of England]], now deposed, lays siege to [[Derry]].
*[[1752]] &amp;ndash; Start of [[Konbaung–Hanthawaddy War]], a new phase in the Burmese Civil War (1740–57)
*[[1770]] &amp;ndash; The [[Georgia (country)|Georgian]] king, [[Erekle II]], abandoned by his [[Russian Empire|Russian]] ally [[Gottlieb Heinrich Totleben|Count Totleben]], wins a victory over [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman forces]] at [[Battle of Aspindza|Aspindza]].
*[[1775]] &amp;ndash; [[American Revolutionary War]]: the [[Siege of Boston]] begins, following the battles at [[Battles of Lexington and Concord|Lexington and Concord]].
*[[1789]] &amp;ndash; President [[George Washington]] arrives in Philadelphia after his inauguration to elaborate welcome at Gray's Ferry just after noon [[first inauguration of George Washington]]
*[[1792]] &amp;ndash; France declares war against the &quot;[[Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor|King of Hungary and Bohemia]]&quot;, the beginning of [[French Revolutionary Wars]].
*[[1809]] &amp;ndash; Two Austrian army corps in Bavaria are defeated by a [[First French Empire]] army led by [[Napoleon I of France]] at the [[Battle of Abensberg]] on the second day of a four-day campaign that ended in a French victory.
*[[1810]] &amp;ndash; The Governor of [[Caracas]] declares independence from Spain.
*[[1818]] &amp;ndash; The case of ''[[Ashford v Thornton]]'' ends, with Abraham Thornton allowed to go free rather than face a retrial for murder, after his demand for [[trial by battle]] is upheld.
*[[1828]] &amp;ndash; [[René Caillié]] becomes the first non-Muslim to enter [[Timbouctou]].
*[[1836]] &amp;ndash; [[Congress of the United States|U.S. Congress]] passes an act creating the [[Wisconsin Territory]].
*[[1861]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Robert E. Lee]] resigns his commission in the [[United States Army]] in order to command the forces of the state of [[Virginia]].
*[[1862]] &amp;ndash; [[Louis Pasteur]] and [[Claude Bernard]] complete the experiment falsifying the theory of [[spontaneous generation]].
*[[1865]] &amp;ndash; Astronomer [[Angelo Secchi|Pietro Angelo Secchi]] demonstrates the [[Secchi disk]], which measures water clarity, aboard [[Pope Pius IX]]'s yacht, the ''L'Immaculata Concezion''.
*[[1871]] &amp;ndash; The [[Civil Rights Act of 1871]] becomes law.
*[[1876]] &amp;ndash; The [[April Uprising]], a key point in modern Bulgarian history, leading to the [[Russo-Turkish War (1877–78)|Russo-Turkish War]] and the liberation of Bulgaria from domination as an independent part of the [[Ottoman Empire]].
*[[1884]] &amp;ndash; [[Pope Leo XIII]] publishes the [[encyclical]] ''[[Humanum Genus]]''.
&lt;!-- Do NOT add the birth of Hitler - it is already in the births section--&gt;
*[[1902]] &amp;ndash; [[Pierre Curie|Pierre]] and [[Maria Sklodowska-Curie|Marie Curie]] refine [[radium]] [[chloride]].
*[[1908]] &amp;ndash; Opening day of competition in the [[New South Wales Rugby League]].
*[[1912]] &amp;ndash; Opening day for [[baseball]]'s [[Tiger Stadium (Detroit)|Tiger Stadium]] in [[Detroit, Michigan|Detroit]], [[Michigan]], and [[Fenway Park]] in [[Boston]].
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; Nineteen men, women, and children die in the [[Ludlow Massacre]] during a [[Colorado]] coal-miner's strike.
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; The [[Chicago Cubs]] play their first game at Weeghman Park (currently [[Wrigley Field]]), defeating the [[Cincinnati Reds]] 7–6 in 11 innings.
*[[1918]] &amp;ndash; [[Manfred von Richthofen]], aka The Red Baron, shoots down his 79th and 80th victims, his final victories before his death the following day.
*[[1922]] &amp;ndash; The [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] government creates [[South Ossetian Autonomous Oblast]] within [[Georgian SSR]].
*[[1926]] &amp;ndash; [[Western Electric]] and [[Warner Bros.]] announce [[Vitaphone]], a process to add sound to film.
*[[1939]] &amp;ndash; [[Adolf Hitler's 50th birthday]] is celebrated as a national holiday in Nazi Germany.
*  1939   &amp;ndash; [[Billie Holiday]] records the first civil rights song &quot;[[Strange Fruit]]&quot;.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: US troops capture [[Leipzig]], Germany, only to later cede the city to the [[Soviet Union]].
*  1945   &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Führerbunker]]: [[Adolf Hitler]] makes his last trip to the surface to award [[Iron Cross]]es to boy soldiers of the [[Hitler Youth]].
*  1945   &amp;ndash; Twenty Jewish children used in medical experiments at [[Neuengamme concentration camp|Neuengamme]] are killed in the basement of the [[Bullenhuser Damm]] school.
*[[1946]] &amp;ndash; The [[League of Nations]] officially dissolves, giving most of its power to the [[United Nations]].
*[[1951]] &amp;ndash; [[Dan Gavriliu]] performs the first surgical replacement of a human organ.
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; Failure of the [[Bay of Pigs Invasion]] of US-backed Cuban exiles against [[Cuba]].
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; [[BBC Two]] launches with a [[power cut]] because of the fire at [[Battersea Power Station]].
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; English politician [[Enoch Powell]] makes his controversial [[Rivers of Blood speech|''Rivers of Blood'' speech]].
*[[1972]] &amp;ndash; [[Apollo 16]], commanded by [[John Young (astronaut)|John Young]], lands on the moon.
*[[1978]] &amp;ndash; [[Korean Air Lines Flight 902]] is shot down by the [[Soviet Union]].
*[[1980]] &amp;ndash; Climax of [[Berber Spring]] in [[Algeria]] as hundreds of [[Berber people|Berber]] political activists are arrested.
*[[1984]] &amp;ndash; The [[Good Friday Massacre]], an extremely violent [[ice hockey]] playoff game, is played in [[Montreal]], Canada.
*[[1985]] &amp;ndash; The [[Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, and Firearms|ATF]] raids [[The Covenant, The Sword, and the Arm of the Lord]] compound in northern [[Arkansas]].
*[[1986]] &amp;ndash; Pianist [[Vladimir Horowitz]] performs in his native Russia for the first time in 61 years.
*[[1998]] &amp;ndash; German terrorist group the [[Red Army Faction]] announces their dissolution after 28 years.
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; [[Columbine High School massacre]]: [[Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold]] killed 13 people and injured 21 others before committing suicide at [[Columbine High School]] in [[Columbine, Colorado]].
*[[2007]] &amp;ndash; [[Johnson Space Center shooting]]: William Phillips with a [[handgun]] barricades himself in [[NASA]]'s [[Johnson Space Center]] in [[Houston]], [[Texas]] before killing a male hostage and himself.
*[[2008]] &amp;ndash; [[Danica Patrick]] wins the [[Indy Japan 300]] becoming the first female driver in history to win an [[American Championship Car Racing|Indy car]] race.
*[[2010]] &amp;ndash; The [[Deepwater Horizon]] drilling rig [[Deepwater Horizon explosion|explodes]] in the Gulf of Mexico, killing eleven workers and beginning an [[Deepwater Horizon oil spill|oil spill that would last six months]].
*[[2012]] &amp;ndash; 127 people are killed when a [[Bhoja Air Flight 213|plane crashes]] in a residential area near the [[Benazir Bhutto International Airport]] near [[Islamabad]], [[Pakistan]].
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; A 6.6-[[Moment magnitude scale|magnitude]] [[2013 Ya'an earthquake|earthquake]] strikes [[Lushan County, Sichuan|Lushan County]], [[Ya'an]], in China's [[Sichuan]] province, killing more than 150 people and injuring thousands.
