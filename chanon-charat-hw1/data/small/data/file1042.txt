==Events==
*  [[25]] &amp;ndash; [[Emperor Guangwu of Han|Guangwu]] claims the throne as [[List of emperors of the Han Dynasty|emperor]] after a period of [[History of the Han Dynasty#Wang Mang's usurpation|political turmoil]], [[History of the Han Dynasty#Reconsolidation under Guangwu|restoring]] the [[Han Dynasty]] after the collapse of the short-lived [[Xin Dynasty]].
* [[642]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Maserfield]]: [[Penda of Mercia]] defeats and kills [[Oswald of Northumbria]].
* [[910]] &amp;ndash; The last major [[Denmark|Danish]] army to raid [[Kingdom of England|England]] is defeated at the [[Battle of Tettenhall]] by the allied forces of [[Mercia]] and [[Wessex]], led by King [[Edward the Elder]] and [[Æthelred, Ealdorman of Mercia|Earl Aethelred of Mercia]].
* [[939]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Alhandic]] is fought between [[Ramiro II of León]] and [[Abd-ar-Rahman III]] at [[Zamora, Spain|Zamora]] in the context of the [[Spanish Reconquista]]. The battle resulted in a victory for the [[Emirate of Cordoba]].
*[[1068]] &amp;ndash; [[Byzantine–Norman wars]]: [[Italo-Norman]]s begin a [[Siege of Bari|nearly-three-year siege]] of [[Bari]].
*[[1100]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry I of England|Henry I]] is crowned [[List of English monarchs|King of England]] in [[Westminster Abbey]].
*[[1278]] &amp;ndash; The [[Siege of Algeciras (1278)|Siege of Algeciras]] ends in the context of the [[Spanish Reconquista]] pitting the forces of the [[Kingdom of Castile]] against the [[Emirate of Granada]]. The battle resulted in a Granadan victory.
*[[1305]] &amp;ndash; [[William Wallace]], who led the [[Scotland|Scottish]] resistance against England, is captured by the English near [[Glasgow]] and transported to [[London]] where he is put on trial and executed.
*[[1388]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Otterburn]], a border skirmish between the [[Scotland|Scottish]] and the [[England|English]] in Northern England, is fought near [[Otterburn, Northumberland|Otterburn]].
*[[1583]] &amp;ndash; Sir [[Humphrey Gilbert]] establishes the first English [[colony]] in [[North America]], at what is now [[St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador]].
*[[1600]] &amp;ndash; The [[John Ruthven, 3rd Earl of Gowrie#Gowrie conspiracy|Gowrie Conspiracy]] against [[James VI and I|King James VI of Scotland]] (later to become King James I of England) takes place.
*[[1620]] &amp;ndash; The ''[[Mayflower]]'' departs from [[Southampton|Southampton, England]] on its first attempt to reach [[North America]].
*[[1689]] &amp;ndash; One thousand five hundred [[Iroquois]] attack the village of [[Lachine, Quebec|Lachine]] in [[New France]].
*[[1716]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Petrovaradin]] takes place.
*[[1735]] &amp;ndash; [[Freedom of the press]]: New York Weekly Journal writer [[John Peter Zenger]] is acquitted of [[seditious libel]] against the royal governor of [[New York]], on the basis that what he had published was true.
*[[1763]] &amp;ndash; [[Pontiac's War]]: [[Battle of Bushy Run]]: [[Great Britain|British]] forces led by [[Henry Bouquet]] defeat [[Chief Pontiac]]'s [[Native Americans in the United States|Indians]] at [[Battle of Bushy Run|Bushy Run]].
*[[1772]] &amp;ndash; The [[First Partition of Poland]] begins.
*[[1781]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Dogger Bank (1781)|Battle of Dogger Bank]] takes place.
*[[1824]] &amp;ndash; [[Greek War of Independence]]: [[Constantine Kanaris]] leads a Greek fleet to victory against Ottoman and Egyptian ships in the [[Battle of Samos (1824)|Battle of Samos]].
*[[1858]] &amp;ndash; [[Cyrus West Field]] and others complete the first [[transatlantic telegraph cable]] after several unsuccessful attempts. It will operate for less than a month.
*[[1860]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles XV of Sweden]] of Sweden-Norway is crowned king of [[Norway]] in [[Trondheim]].
*[[1861]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: In order to help pay for the war effort, the [[Federal government of the United States|United States government]] levies the first [[income tax]] as part of the [[Revenue Act of 1861]] (3% of all incomes over US $800; rescinded in [[1872]]).
*  1861   &amp;ndash; The [[United States Army]] abolishes [[Flagellation|flogging]].
*[[1862]] &amp;ndash; American Civil War: [[Battle of Baton Rouge (1862)|Battle of Baton Rouge]]: Along the [[Mississippi River]] near [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]], [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] troops attempt to take the city, but are driven back by fire from Union gunboats.
*[[1864]] &amp;ndash; American Civil War: The [[Battle of Mobile Bay]] begins at [[Mobile Bay]] near [[Mobile, Alabama]], [[Admiral]] [[David Farragut]] leads a Union flotilla through Confederate defenses and seals one of the last major Southern ports.
*[[1874]] &amp;ndash; [[Japan]] launches its [[postal savings system]], modeled after a similar system in the [[United Kingdom]].
*[[1882]] &amp;ndash; The [[Esso|Standard Oil of New Jersey]] is established.
*[[1884]] &amp;ndash; The cornerstone for the [[Statue of Liberty]] is laid on [[Liberty Island|Bedloe's Island]] (now Liberty Island) in [[New York Harbor]].
*[[1888]] &amp;ndash; [[Bertha Benz]] drives from [[Mannheim]] to [[Pforzheim]] and back in the first long distance automobile trip, commemorated as the [[Bertha Benz Memorial Route]] since 2008.
*[[1901]] &amp;ndash; [[Peter O'Connor (athlete)|Peter O'Connor]] sets the first [[International Association of Athletics Federations|IAAF]] recognised [[long jump]] [[List of world records in athletics|world record]] of {{convert|24|ft|11.75|in|m|abbr=on}}.  The record will stand for 20 years.
*[[1906]] &amp;ndash; [[Persian Constitutional Revolution]]: [[Mozaffar ad-Din Shah Qajar]], King of Iran, agrees to convert the government to a [[constitutional monarchy]].
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: The German [[minelayer]] {{SS|Königin Luise|1913|6}} lays a minefield about {{convert|40|mi|km}} off the [[Thames Estuary]] ([[Lowestoft]]). She is intercepted and sunk by the British light-cruiser {{HMS|Amphion|1911|6}}.
*  1914   &amp;ndash; World War I: The guns of Point Nepean fort at Port Phillip Heads in Victoria (Australia) fire across the bows of the Norddeutscher Lloyd steamer {{SS|Pfalz|1913|6}} which is attempting to leave the Port of Melbourne in ignorance of the declaration of war and she is detained; this is said to be the first Allied shot of the War.
*  1914   &amp;ndash; In [[Cleveland|Cleveland, Ohio]], the first electric [[traffic light]] is installed.
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; World War I: [[Battle of Romani]]: Allied forces, under the command of [[Archibald Murray]], defeat an attacking [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman army]] under the command of [[Friedrich Freiherr Kress von Kressenstein]], securing the [[Suez Canal]] and beginning the Ottoman retreat from the [[Sinai Peninsula]].
*[[1925]] &amp;ndash; [[Plaid Cymru]] is formed with the aim of disseminating knowledge of the [[Welsh language]] that is at the time in danger of dying out.
*[[1926]] &amp;ndash; [[Harry Houdini]] performs his greatest feat, spending 91 minutes underwater in a sealed tank before escaping.
*[[1940]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: The [[Soviet Union]] formally [[Soviet occupation of Latvia in 1940#1940-1941: The first Soviet occupation|annexes]] [[Latvia]].
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; World War II: The [[Battle of Smolensk (1941)|Battle of Smolensk]] concludes with Germany capturing about 300,000 Soviet [[Red Army]] prisoners.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; World War II: Possibly the biggest prison breakout in history occurs as 545 [[Japanese people|Japanese]] [[Prisoner of war|POW]]s [[Cowra breakout|attempt to escape]] outside the town of [[Cowra]], [[New South Wales]], [[Australia]].
*  1944   &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Poland|Polish]] insurgents liberate a [[Germany|German]] [[labor camp]] in [[Warsaw]], freeing 348 [[Jews|Jewish]] prisoners.
*  1944   &amp;ndash; World War II: The Nazis begin a [[Wola massacre|week-long massacre]] of anywhere between 40,000 and 100,000 civilians and prisoners of war in [[Wola]], [[Poland]].
*[[1949]] &amp;ndash; In [[Ecuador]], [[1949 Ambato earthquake|an earthquake]] destroys 50 towns and kills more than 6,000.
*  1949   &amp;ndash; The [[Mann Gulch fire]] kills 13 firefighters in [[Montana]].
*[[1957]] &amp;ndash; ''[[American Bandstand]]'', a show dedicated to the teenage &quot;baby-boomers&quot; by playing the songs and showing popular dances of the time, debuts on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] television network.
*[[1958]] &amp;ndash; [[Herbert Hoover]] eclipses [[John Adams]] as having the longest retirement of any former U.S President until that time.  Hoover would live another six years, his record 31 years 7 months 16 days retirement has since been eclipsed by Jimmy Carter.
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; [[Burkina Faso]], then known as [[Republic of Upper Volta|Upper Volta]], becomes independent from [[France]].
*[[1962]] &amp;ndash; [[Apartheid]] in [[South Africa]]: [[Nelson Mandela]] is jailed. He would not be released until [[1990]].
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States]], the [[United Kingdom]], and the Soviet Union sign a [[Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty|nuclear test ban treaty]].
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[Operation Pierce Arrow]]: [[United States|American]] aircraft from carriers {{USS|Ticonderoga|CV-14|6}} and {{USS|Constellation|CV-64|6}} bomb [[North Vietnam]] in retaliation for strikes against U.S. destroyers in the [[Gulf of Tonkin]].
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; The [[Indo-Pakistani War of 1965]] begins as Pakistani soldiers cross the [[Line of Control]] dressed as locals.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; [[Mariner program]]: ''[[Mariner 6 and 7|Mariner 7]]'' makes its closest fly-by of [[Mars]] (3,524 kilometers).
*[[1971]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Pacific Islands Forum]] (then known as the &quot;South Pacific Forum&quot;) is held in [[Wellington]], [[New Zealand]], with the aim of enhancing cooperation between the independent countries of the Pacific Ocean.
*[[1974]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[United States Congress|U.S. Congress]] places a $1 billion limit on military aid to [[South Vietnam]].
*[[1979]] &amp;ndash; In [[Afghanistan]], [[Afghanistan Liberation Organization|Maoists]] undertake an attempted military uprising.
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; President [[Ronald Reagan]] [[Professional Air Traffic Controllers Organization (1968)|fires]] 11,359 [[Strike action|striking]] air-traffic controllers who ignored his order for them to return to work.
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; General elections are held in [[Nicaragua]] with the [[Sandinista National Liberation Front]] winning a majority.
*[[1995]] &amp;ndash; [[Yugoslav Wars]]: The city of [[Knin]], Croatia, a significant [[Serbs|Serb]] stronghold, is captured by [[Croatia]]n forces during [[Operation Storm]]. The date is celebrated in Croatia as [[Victory Day (Croatia)|Victory Day]].
*[[2010]] &amp;ndash; The [[2010 Copiapó mining accident|Copiapó mining accident]] occurs, trapping 33 Chilean miners approximately {{convert|2,300|ft|m|abbr=on}} below the ground.
*  2010   &amp;ndash; Ten members of [[International Assistance Mission]] Nuristan Eye Camp team [[2010 Badakhshan massacre|are killed]] by persons unknown in [[Kuran wa Munjan District]] of [[Badakhshan Province]] in [[Afghanistan]].
