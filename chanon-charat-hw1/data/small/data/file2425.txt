'''Berinthia''' &quot;'''Berry'''&quot; '''Berenson Perkins''' (April 14, 1948 – September 11, 2001) was an American photographer, actress, and model. Perkins, who was the widow of actor [[Anthony Perkins]], died in the [[September 11 attacks]] as a passenger on [[American Airlines Flight 11]].

==Early life==
Berenson was born in [[Murray Hill, Manhattan]]. Her father, Robert Lawrence Berenson, was an American career diplomat turned shipping executive; he was of [[Lithuanian Jews|Lithuanian Jewish]] descent, and his family's original surname was Valvrojenski.&lt;ref&gt;Bernard Berenson. ''Sketch for a Self-Portrait''. New York. Pantheon. 1949&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;&quot;Robert L. Berenson, Ex-Envoy and Head of Shipping Line, Dies&quot;. ''[[The New York Times]]''. February 3, 1965, page 35&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;[http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=FB0A15F93859167B93CAA91788D85F458485F9 &quot;Marisa $chiaparelli Is Married in Gown Designed. by Her Mother, the Cougurlere&quot;]. ''The New York Times''.&lt;/ref&gt; Her mother was born  Maria-Luisa Yvonne Radha de Wendt de Kerlor, better known as [[Elsa Schiaparelli#Family|Gogo Schiaparelli]], a socialite of [[Italian people|Italian]], [[Swiss people|Swiss]], [[French people|French]], and [[Egyptian people|Egyptian]] ancestry.&lt;ref name = &quot;htgxkf&quot;&gt;Elsa Schiaparelli. ''Shocking Life''. New York. Dutton, 1954&lt;/ref&gt;

Her maternal grandmother was the Italian-born fashion designer [[Elsa Schiaparelli]],&lt;ref&gt;Linda Greenhouse, &quot;Schiaparelli Dies in Paris; Brought Color to Fashion&quot;, The New York Times, 15 November 1973&lt;/ref&gt; and her maternal grandfather was Wilhelm de Wendt de Kerlor, a [[Theosophy|Theosophist]] and [[psychic medium]].&lt;ref name = &quot;htgxkf&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|last=Thurman |first=Judith |url=http://www.newyorker.com/archive/2003/10/27/031027fa_fact_thurman |title=Mother of Invention|publisher=[[The New Yorker]]|date=October 27, 2003}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.ritmanlibrary.nl/c/p/h/bel_18.html |title=Bibliotheca Philosophica Hermetica entry |publisher=Ritmanlibrary.nl |date=2006-02-27 |accessdate=2010-11-12}}&lt;/ref&gt; Her elder sister, [[Marisa Berenson]], became a well-known model and actress. She also was a great-grandniece of [[Giovanni Schiaparelli]], an Italian astronomer who believed he had discovered the supposed [[Martian canal|canals of Mars]], and a second cousin, once removed, of art expert [[Bernard Berenson]] (1865–1959) and his sister [[Senda Berenson Abbott|Senda Berenson]] (1868–1954), an athlete and educator who was one of the first two women elected to the Basketball Hall of Fame.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9125376/Senda-Berenson#668134.hook |title=Encyclopædia Britannica Online entry |publisher=Britannica.com |date=1954-02-16 |accessdate=2010-11-12}}&lt;/ref&gt;

==Career==
Following a brief modeling career in the late 1960s, Berenson became a freelance photographer. By 1973, her photographs had been published in ''[[Life (magazine)|Life]]'', ''[[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]]'', ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' and ''[[Newsweek]]''.&lt;ref&gt;[[Judy Klemesrud]], &quot;And Now, Make Room for the Berenson Sisters&quot;, The New York Times, 19 April 1973, page 54&lt;/ref&gt;

She also appeared in several motion pictures, including ''[[Cat People (1982 film)|Cat People]]'' with [[Malcolm McDowell]]. She starred opposite [[Anthony Perkins]] in the 1978 [[Alan Rudolph]] film ''[[Remember My Name (film)|Remember My Name]]'' and opposite [[Jeff Bridges]] in the 1979 film ''[[Winter Kills (film)|Winter Kills]]''.

==Personal life and death==
On August 9, 1973, in [[Cape Cod]], [[Massachusetts]], Berenson married her ''Remember My Name'' costar Anthony Perkins. They had two sons: actor-musician [[Oz Perkins]] (born February 2, 1974) and folk/rock recording artist [[Elvis Perkins]] (born February 9, 1976).&lt;ref&gt;Maynard, Joyce (January 12, 1977). &quot;Tony Perkins and Family: A Study in Informal Togetherness&quot;. ''The New York Times''. Westchester Weekly Section. p. 58&lt;/ref&gt; They remained married until Perkins' death from [[AIDS]]-related complications on September 12, 1992.&lt;ref&gt;Goodman, Mark (September 28, 1992). [http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20108694,00.html &quot;One Final Myster: Surrounded by Family, Friends and a Wall of Silence, Tony Perkins Succumbs to AIDS&quot;]. ''People''. Vol. 38 No. 13.&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;Weinraub, Bernard (September 16, 1992). [http://www.nytimes.com/1992/09/16/arts/anthony-perkins-s-wife-tells-of-2-years-of-secrecy.html &quot;Anthony Perkins's Wife Tells of 2 Years of Secrecy&quot;]. ''The New York Times''.&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;Ferrell, David (September 13, 1992). [http://articles.latimes.com/1992-09-13/news/mn-1407_1_anthony-perkins &quot;Anthony Perkins, 60, Dies; Star of 'Psycho' Had AIDS&quot;]. ''[[Los Angeles Times]]''.&lt;/ref&gt;

Berenson died at age 53 in the [[September 11 attacks]] aboard [[American Airlines Flight 11]], one day before the ninth anniversary of Perkins' death. She was returning to her [[California]] home following a holiday on [[Cape Cod]].&lt;ref&gt;Hopkinson, Amanda (September 14, 2001). [http://www.theguardian.com/news/2001/sep/14/guardianobituaries.september11 &quot;Berry Berenson&quot;]. ''The Guardian''.&lt;/ref&gt;

At the [[National September 11 Memorial &amp; Museum]], Berenson is memorialized at the North Pool, on Panel N-76.&lt;ref&gt;[http://names.911memorial.org/#lang=en_US&amp;page=person&amp;id=4444 &quot;Berry Berenson Perkins&quot;]. Memorial Guide: [[National 9/11 Memorial]]. Retrieved October 28, 2011.&lt;/ref&gt;{{-}}
