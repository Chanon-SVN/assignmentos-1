==Events==
*[[404 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Peloponnesian War]]: [[Lysander]]'s [[Sparta]]n Armies defeated the [[Classical Athens|Athenians]] and the war ends.
* [[775]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Bagrevand]] puts an end to an [[Armenians|Armenian]] rebellion against the [[Abbasid Caliphate]]. Muslim control over [[Transcaucasia]] is solidified and its Islamization begins, while several major Armenian ''[[nakharar]]'' families lose power and their remnants flee to the [[Byzantine Empire]].
*[[1134]] &amp;ndash; The name [[Zagreb]] was mentioned for the first time in the Felician Charter relating to the establishment of the Zagreb Bishopric around 1094.
*[[1607]] &amp;ndash; [[Eighty Years' War]]: The [[Netherlands|Dutch]] fleet [[Battle of Gibraltar (1607)|destroys]] the anchored Spanish fleet at [[Gibraltar]].
*[[1644]] &amp;ndash; The [[Chongzhen Emperor]], the last Emperor of [[Ming Dynasty]] China, commits suicide during a peasant rebellion led by [[Li Zicheng]].
*[[1707]] &amp;ndash; The [[House of Habsburg|Habsburg]] army is defeated by [[House of Bourbon|Bourbon]] army at [[Battle of Almansa|Almansa]] (Spain) in the [[War of the Spanish Succession]].
*[[1792]] &amp;ndash; [[Highwayman]] [[Nicolas Jacques Pelletier|Nicolas J. Pelletier]] becomes the first person executed by [[guillotine]].
*  1792   &amp;ndash; ''[[La Marseillaise]]'' (the French [[national anthem]]) is composed by [[Claude Joseph Rouget de Lisle]].
*[[1804]] &amp;ndash; The western Georgian [[kingdom of Imereti]] accepts the [[suzerainty]] of the [[Russian Empire]]
*[[1829]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles Fremantle]] arrives in HMS Challenger off the coast of modern-day Western Australia prior to declaring the [[Swan River Colony]] for the United Kingdom.
*[[1846]] &amp;ndash; [[Thornton Affair]]: Open conflict begins over the disputed border of [[Texas]], triggering the [[Mexican–American War]].
*[[1847]] &amp;ndash; The last survivors of the [[Donner Party]] are out of the wilderness.
*[[1849]] &amp;ndash; The [[Governor General of Canada]], [[Lord Elgin]], signs the [[Rebellion Losses Bill]], outraging [[Montreal]]'s [[English-Canadian|English]] population and triggering the [[Montreal Riots]].
*[[1859]] &amp;ndash; British and French engineers break ground for the [[Suez Canal]].
*[[1862]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: Forces under [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] [[Admiral]] [[David Farragut]] [[Capture of New Orleans|demand the surrender]] of the [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] city of [[New Orleans|New Orleans, Louisiana]].
*[[1864]] &amp;ndash; American Civil War: The [[Battle of Marks' Mills]].
*[[1882]] &amp;ndash; [[Tonkin Campaign]]: [[French Third Republic|French]] and [[Nguyễn Dynasty|Vietnamese]] troops clashed in [[Tonkin]], when Commandant [[Henri Rivière (naval officer)|Henri Rivière]] [[Battle of Hanoi (1882)|seized]] the [[citadel of Hanoi]] with a small force of marine infantry.
*[[1898]] &amp;ndash; [[Spanish–American War]]: The United States declares war on Spain.
*[[1901]] &amp;ndash; New York becomes the first [[U.S. state]] to require [[automobile]] [[license plate]]s.&lt;br/&gt;[[File:Anzac Beach 4th Bn landing 8am April 25 1915.jpg|thumb|right|Anzac Beach &amp;ndash; Australian and New Zealand forces invade Turkey]]
*[[1915]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: The [[Battle of Gallipoli]] begins—The invasion of the Turkish Gallipoli Peninsula by Australian, British, French and New Zealand troops begins with landings at [[Landing at Anzac Cove|Anzac Cove]] and [[Landing at Cape Helles|Cape Helles]].
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; [[Easter Rebellion]]: The United Kingdom declares [[martial law]] in Ireland.
*  1916   &amp;ndash; [[Anzac Day]] is commemorated for the first time on the first anniversary of the landing at Anzac Cove.
*[[1920]] &amp;ndash; At the [[San Remo conference]], the principal [[Allies of World War I|Allied Powers of World War I]] adopt a resolution to determine the allocation of Class &quot;A&quot; [[League of Nations mandate]]s for administration of the former [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman]]-ruled lands of the Middle East.
*[[1938]] &amp;ndash; [[U.S. Supreme Court]] delivers its opinion in ''[[Erie Railroad Co. v. Tompkins]]'' and overturns a century of federal common law.
*[[1943]] &amp;ndash; The [[Demyansk Shield]] for German troops in commemoration of [[Demyansk Pocket]] is instituted.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; The [[United Negro College Fund]] is incorporated.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; [[Elbe Day]]: [[United States Armed Forces|United States]] and [[Soviet Armed Forces|Soviet]] troops meet in [[Torgau]] along the River [[Elbe]], cutting the ''[[Wehrmacht]]'' of [[Nazi Germany]] in two, a milestone in the approaching [[end of World War II in Europe]].
*  1945   &amp;ndash; [[Liberation Day (Italy)]]: The [[Nazi Germany|Nazi]] occupation army surrenders and leaves Northern Italy after a general partisan insurrection by the [[Italian resistance movement]]; the puppet [[Italian Social Republic|fascist regime]] dissolves and [[Benito Mussolini]] is captured after trying to escape. This day was set as a [[Public holidays in Italy|public holiday]] to celebrate the Liberation of Italy.
*  1945   &amp;ndash; Fifty nations gather in [[San Francisco|San Francisco, California]] to begin the [[United Nations Conference on International Organization]].
*  1945   &amp;ndash; The last German troops retreat from Finland's soil in Lapland, ending the [[Lapland War]]. Military acts of Second World War end in Finland.
*[[1951]] &amp;ndash; [[Korean War]]: Assaulting Chinese forces are forced to withdraw after heavy fighting with UN forces, primarily made up of Australian and Canadian troops, at the [[Battle of Kapyong]].
*[[1953]] &amp;ndash; [[Francis Crick]] and [[James D. Watson]] publish &quot;[[Molecular Structure of Nucleic Acids: A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid]]&quot; describing the [[double helix]] structure of [[DNA]].
*[[1954]] &amp;ndash; The first practical [[Solar cell#History of solar cells|solar cell]] is publicly demonstrated by [[Bell Labs|Bell Telephone Laboratories]].
*[[1959]] &amp;ndash; The [[St. Lawrence Seaway]], linking the North American [[Great Lakes (North America)|Great Lakes]] and the Atlantic Ocean, officially opens to [[shipping]].
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States Navy|U.S. Navy]] submarine {{USS|Triton|SSRN-586|6}} completes the [[Operation Sandblast|first submerged circumnavigation of the globe]].
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; [[Robert Noyce]] is granted a [[patent]] for an [[integrated circuit]].
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; Teenage [[sniper]] [[Highway 101 Snipe attack -1965|Michael Andrew Clark]] kills three and wounds six others shooting from a hilltop along [[Highway 101]] just south of [[Santa Maria, California]].
*[[1966]] &amp;ndash; The city of [[Tashkent]] is destroyed by a huge earthquake.
*[[1972]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: Nguyen Hue Offensive &amp;ndash; The [[North Vietnam]]ese 320th Division forces 5,000 [[South Vietnam]]ese troops to retreat and traps about 2,500 others northwest of [[Kontum]].
*[[1974]] &amp;ndash; [[Carnation Revolution]]: A leftist military [[coup]] in Portugal overthrows the fascist ''[[Estado Novo (Portugal)|Estado Novo]]'' regime and establishes a democratic government.
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; As [[North Vietnamese Army|North Vietnamese]] forces close in on the [[South Vietnam]]ese [[capital (political)|capital]] [[Saigon]], the Australian [[Embassy]] is closed and evacuated, almost ten years to the day since the first Australian troop commitment to [[South Vietnam]].
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; More than 100 workers are exposed to radiation during repairs of a [[nuclear power plant]] in [[Tsuruga, Fukui|Tsuruga]], Japan.
*[[1982]] &amp;ndash; [[Israel]] completes its withdrawal from the [[Sinai Peninsula]] per the [[Camp David Accords]].
*[[1983]] &amp;ndash; American [[student|schoolgirl]] [[Samantha Smith]] is invited to visit the [[Soviet Union]] by its leader [[Yuri Andropov]] after he read her letter in which she expressed fears about [[nuclear warfare|nuclear war]].
*  1983   &amp;ndash; ''[[Pioneer 10]]'' travels beyond [[Pluto]]'s [[orbit]].
*[[1986]] &amp;ndash; [[Mswati III of Swaziland|Mswati III]] is crowned [[List of kings of Swaziland|King of Swaziland]], succeeding his father [[Sobhuza II of Swaziland|Sobhuza II]].
*[[1988]] &amp;ndash; In [[Israel]], [[John Demjanuk]] is sentenced to death for [[war crime]]s committed in World War II.
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; [[Violeta Chamorro]] takes office as the [[President of Nicaragua]], the first woman to hold the position.
*[[2001]] &amp;ndash; [[Michele Alboreto]] is killed while testing an [[Audi R8 (race car)|Audi R8]] at the [[EuroSpeedway Lausitz|Lausitzring]] in Germany.
*[[2005]] &amp;ndash; The final piece of the [[Obelisk of Axum]] is returned to [[Ethiopia]] after being stolen by the invading Italian army in [[1937]].
*  2005   &amp;ndash; [[Bulgaria]] and [[Romania]] sign accession treaties to join the [[European Union]].
*  2005   &amp;ndash; One hundred seven people die in [[Amagasaki rail crash]] in Japan.
*[[2007]] &amp;ndash; [[Boris Yeltsin]]'s funeral &amp;ndash; the first to be sanctioned by the [[Russian Orthodox Church]] for a head of state since the funeral of Emperor [[Alexander III of Russia|Alexander III]] in 1894.
