The '''Dasyproctidae''' are a family of large [[South America]]n [[rodent]]s, comprising the [[Common agouti|agouti]]s and [[acouchi]]s.&lt;ref name = MSW3&gt;{{MSW3 Hystricognathi | id = 13400223 | page = }}&lt;/ref&gt; Their fur is a reddish or dark colour above, with a paler underside. They are [[herbivore|herbivorous]], often feeding on ripe [[fruit]] that falls from trees. They live in burrows, and, like [[squirrel]]s, will bury some of their food for later use.&lt;ref name=EoM&gt;{{cite book |editor=Macdonald, D.|author= Bishop, Ian|year=1984 |title= The Encyclopedia of Mammals|publisher= Facts on File|location=New York|page= 701|isbn= 0-87196-871-1}}&lt;/ref&gt;
