'''Alexey Leonidovich Pajitnov''' ({{lang-ru|Алексе́й Леони́дович Па́житнов}}, {{transl|ru|ISO|Alekséi Leonídovich Pázhitnov}};&lt;!-- but he prefers the spelling Pajitnov, as explained on the talk page --&gt; born 14 March 1956) is a Russian [[video game designer]] and [[Computer engineering|computer engineer]], who developed the popular game ''[[Tetris]]'' while working for the [[Dorodnitsyn Computing Centre]] of the [[Russian Academy of Sciences|Soviet Academy of Sciences]], a [[Soviet Union|Soviet government]]-founded [[Research and development|R&amp;D]] center.

He only started to get royalties from his creation in 1996 when he and [[Henk Rogers]] formed [[The Tetris Company]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.atarihq.com/tsr/special/tetrishist.html |title=Tetris: a history |publisher=Atarihq.com |date= |accessdate=2012-05-06}}&lt;/ref&gt;

==Biography==
Alexey Pajitnov created ''Tetris'' with the help of Dmitry Pavlovsky and [[Vadim Gerasimov]] in 1984. The game, first available in the Soviet Union, appeared in the West in 1986.

Pajitnov also created the lesser known sequel to ''Tetris'', entitled ''[[Welltris]]'', which has the same principle but in a [[3D computer graphics|three dimensional]] environment where the player sees the playing area from above.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mds.mdh.se/~frv95pen/game/tetris/welltris/ |title=Screenshots from Welltris, retrieved 31-10-2007 |publisher=Mds.mdh.se |date= |accessdate=2009-11-20}}&lt;/ref&gt; ''Tetris'' was licensed and managed by Soviet company [[ELORG]] which had been founded specially for this purpose, and advertised with the slogan ''&quot;From Russia with Love&quot;'' (on NES: ''&quot;From Russia With Fun!&quot;''). Because he was employed by the Soviet government, Pajitnov did not receive royalties.

Pajitnov, together with [[Vladimir Pokhilko]], moved to the [[United States]] in 1991 and later, in 1996, founded The Tetris Company with Henk Rogers. He helped design the puzzles in the [[Super Nintendo Entertainment System|Super NES]] versions of ''[[Yoshi's Cookie]]'' and designed the game ''[[Pandora's Box (1999 video game)|Pandora's Box]]'', which incorporates more traditional [[jigsaw puzzle|jigsaw-style puzzles]].

He was employed by [[Microsoft]] from October 1996 until 2005. While there he worked on the ''[[Microsoft Entertainment Pack: The Puzzle Collection]]'', MSN Mind Aerobics and [[MSN Games]] groups. Pajitnov's new, enhanced version of ''[[Hexic]]'', ''Hexic HD'', was included with every new [[Xbox 360]] Premium package.

On August 18, 2005, WildSnake Software announced Pajitnov will be collaborating with them to release a new line of puzzle games.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.wildsnake.com/newsletter/2005-08-18/ |title=WildSnake newsletter august 18 2005, retrieved 31-10-2007 |publisher=Wildsnake.com |date=2005-08-18 |accessdate=2009-11-20| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20091112184900/http://www.wildsnake.com/newsletter/2005-08-18/| archivedate= 12 November 2009 &lt;!--DASHBot--&gt;| deadurl= no}}&lt;/ref&gt;

==Awards and recognition==
*In 1996, [[GameSpot]] named him as the fourth most influential [[PC game|computer game]] developer of all time.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://web.archive.org/web/20050221115412/http://www.gamespot.com/features/15most/html/mi_04.html |title=Internet Archive Wayback Machine |publisher=Web.archive.org |date=2005-02-21 |accessdate=2012-05-06}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*On March 7, 2007, he received the [[Game Developers Choice Awards#The Pioneer Award|Game Developers Choice Awards First Penguin Award]]. The award was given for pioneering the casual games market.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=http://www.gamasutra.com/php-bin/news_index.php?story=12732 |title=2007 Game Developers Choice Awards To Honor Miyamoto, Pajitnov |publisher=[[Gamasutra]] |accessdate=2007-02-12| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20070307054731/http://www.gamasutra.com/php-bin/news_index.php?story=12732| archivedate= 7 March 2007 &lt;!--DASHBot--&gt;| deadurl= no}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*On June 24, 2009, he  received the honorary award at the LARA - Der Deutsche Games Award in [[Cologne|Cologne, Germany]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url= http://www.heise.de/newsticker/Games-Award-ehrt-Tetris-Erfinder-Paschitnow--/meldung/141061|title=News report on Deutsche Games Award 2009 |publisher=[[Heinz Heise|Heise.de]]|accessdate=2009-06-25| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20090626082322/http://www.heise.de/newsticker/Games-Award-ehrt-Tetris-Erfinder-Paschitnow--/meldung/141061| archivedate= 26 June 2009 &lt;!--DASHBot--&gt;| deadurl= no}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*In 2012, [[IGN]] included Pajitnov on their list of 5 Memorable Video Game Industry One-Hit Wonders, calling him &quot;the ultimate video game one-hit wonder.&quot;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|first=Luke|last=Reilly|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2012/04/29/5-memorable-video-game-industry-one-hit-wonders|title=5 Memorable Video Game Industry One-Hit Wonders - Games Feature at IGN |publisher=IGN|accessdate=2012-05-06}}&lt;/ref&gt;
