==Events==
* [[357]] &amp;ndash; Emperor [[Constantius II]] enters [[Rome]] for the first time to celebrate his victory over [[Magnentius|Magnus Magnentius]].
*[[1192]] &amp;ndash; [[Assassination]] of [[Conrad of Montferrat]] (Conrad I), [[Kings of Jerusalem|King of Jerusalem]], in [[Tyre (Lebanon)|Tyre]], two days after his title to the [[throne]] is confirmed by election. The killing is carried out by [[Hashshashin]].
*[[1253]] &amp;ndash; [[Nichiren]], a [[Japan]]ese [[Buddhism|Buddhist]] monk, propounds ''[[Nam Myoho Renge Kyo]]'' for the very first time and declares it to be the essence of Buddhism, in effect founding [[Nichiren Buddhism]].
*[[1503]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Cerignola]] is fought. It is noted as the first battle in history won by small arms fire using [[gunpowder]].
*[[1611]] &amp;ndash; Establishment of the Pontifical and Royal [[University of Santo Tomas]], The Catholic University of the Philippines, the largest Catholic university in the world.
*[[1788]] &amp;ndash; [[Maryland]] becomes the seventh state to ratify the [[Constitution of the United States]].
*[[1789]] &amp;ndash; [[Mutiny on the Bounty|Mutiny on the ''Bounty'']]: Lieutenant [[William Bligh]] and 18 sailors are set adrift and the rebel crew returns to [[Tahiti]] briefly and then sets sail for [[Pitcairn Island]].
*[[1792]] &amp;ndash; [[France]] invades the [[Austrian Netherlands#Austrian Netherlands|Austrian Netherlands]] (present day [[Belgium]]), beginning the [[French Revolutionary War]].
*[[1796]] &amp;ndash; The [[Armistice of Cherasco]] is signed by [[Napoleon I of France|Napoleon Bonaparte]] and [[Victor Amadeus III of Sardinia|Vittorio Amedeo III]], the [[King of Sardinia]], expanding [[France|French]] territory along the [[Mediterranean Sea|Mediterranean]] coast.
*[[1869]] &amp;ndash; Chinese and Irish laborers for the [[Central Pacific Railroad]] working on the [[First Transcontinental Railroad]] lay 10 miles of track in one day, a feat which has never been matched.
*[[1887]] &amp;ndash; A week after being arrested by the [[Prussian Secret Police]], [[Alsace|Alsatian]] police inspector [[Guillaume Schnaebelé]] is released on order of [[German Emperor]] [[William I, German Emperor|William I]], defusing a possible war.
*[[1910]] &amp;ndash; Frenchman [[Louis Paulhan]] wins the [[1910 London to Manchester air race]], the first long-distance aeroplane race in England.
*[[1920]] &amp;ndash; [[Azerbaijan]] is added to the [[Soviet Union]].
*[[1930]] &amp;ndash; The first night game in organized [[baseball]] history takes place in [[Independence, Kansas]].
*[[1932]] &amp;ndash; A [[vaccine]] for [[yellow fever]] is announced for use on [[human]]s.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: Nine German [[E-boat]]s attacked US and UK units during [[Exercise Tiger]], the rehearsal for the [[Normandy landings]], killing 946.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; [[Benito Mussolini]] and his mistress [[Clara Petacci]] are executed by a firing squad consisting of members of the [[Italian resistance movement]].
*[[1947]] &amp;ndash; [[Thor Heyerdahl]] and five crew mates set out from [[Peru]] on the ''[[Kon-Tiki]]'' to prove that Peruvian natives could have settled [[Polynesia]].
*[[1948]] &amp;ndash; [[Igor Stravinsky]] conducted the premier of his American ballet, ''[[Orpheus (ballet)|Orpheus]]'', in New York City at New York City Center.
*[[1949]] &amp;ndash; Former [[First Lady of the Philippines]] [[Aurora Quezon]], 61, is assassinated while ''en route'' to dedicate a hospital in memory of her [[Manuel L. Quezon|late husband]]; her daughter and ten others are also killed.
*[[1950]] &amp;ndash; [[Bhumibol Adulyadej]] marries [[Queen Sirikit]] after their quiet engagement in [[Lausanne]], [[Switzerland]] on July 19, 1949.
*[[1952]] &amp;ndash; [[Dwight D. Eisenhower]] resigns as [[Supreme Allied Commander]] of [[NATO]].
* 1952 &amp;ndash; [[Occupied Japan]]: The [[United States]] [[occupation of Japan]] ends as the [[Treaty of San Francisco]], ratified September 8, 1951, comes into force.
* 1952 &amp;ndash; The [[Treaty of Taipei|Sino-Japanese Peace Treaty (Treaty of Taipei)]] is signed in [[Taipei]], [[Taiwan]] between Japan and the [[Republic of China]] to officially end the [[Second Sino-Japanese War]].
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; [[1965 United States occupation of the Dominican Republic|United States occupation of the Dominican Republic]]: American troops land in the [[Dominican Republic]] to &quot;forestall establishment of a [[Communist]] dictatorship&quot; and to evacuate [[United States Army|U.S. Army]] troops.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles de Gaulle]] resigns as [[President of France]].
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Richard M. Nixon]] formally authorizes American combat troops to fight [[communist]] sanctuaries in [[Cambodia]].
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; General [[Cao Van Vien]], chief of the [[Army of the Republic of Vietnam|South Vietnamese military]], departs for the US as the [[North Vietnamese Army]] [[Ho Chi Minh Campaign|closed in on victory]].
*[[1977]] &amp;ndash; The [[Red Army Faction]] trial ends, with [[Andreas Baader]], [[Gudrun Ensslin]] and [[Jan-Carl Raspe]] found guilty of four counts of [[murder]] and more than 30 counts of [[attempted murder]].
*  1977   &amp;ndash; The [[Budapest Treaty|Budapest Treaty on the International Recognition of the Deposit of Microorganisms for the Purposes of Patent Procedure]] is signed.
*[[1978]] &amp;ndash; [[President of Afghanistan]], [[Mohammed Daoud Khan]], is overthrown and [[assassination|assassinated]] in a [[Saur Revolution|coup]] led by pro-communist rebels.
*[[1986]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States Navy]] [[aircraft carrier]] {{USS|Enterprise|CVN-65|6}} becomes the first nuclear-powered [[aircraft carrier]] to transit the [[Suez Canal]], navigating from the [[Red Sea]] to the Mediterranean Sea to relieve the {{USS|Coral Sea|CV-43|6}}.
*  1986   &amp;ndash; High levels of radiation resulting from the [[Chernobyl disaster]] are detected at a nuclear power plant in [[Sweden]], leading Soviet authorities to publicly announce the accident.
*[[1987]] &amp;ndash; American engineer [[Ben Linder]] is killed in an ambush by U.S.-funded [[Contra (guerrillas)|Contras]] in northern [[Nicaragua]].
*[[1988]] &amp;ndash; Near [[Maui, Hawaii]], [[flight attendant]] Clarabelle &quot;C.B.&quot; Lansing is blown out of [[Aloha Airlines Flight 243]], a [[Boeing 737]], and falls to her death when part of the plane's fuselage rips open in mid-flight.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; Former [[Central Intelligence Agency]] [[counter-intelligence]] officer and analyst [[Aldrich Ames]] pleads guilty to giving U.S. secrets to the [[Soviet Union]] and later [[Russia]].
*[[1996]] &amp;ndash; [[Whitewater controversy]]: President [[Bill Clinton]] gives a 4½ hour videotaped testimony for the defense.
*[[2001]] &amp;ndash; [[Millionaire]] [[Dennis Tito]] becomes the world's first [[space tourism|space tourist]].
